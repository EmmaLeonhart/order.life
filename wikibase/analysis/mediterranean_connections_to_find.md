# Ancient Mediterranean Connections to Investigate

Points of interest in the wikibase genealogy where distinct cultural
traditions converge. These are narratively important for the Gaiad's
deflationary polytheism — showing continuity where traditions are
usually treated as separate.

## Known connections (confirmed in data)

### Jezebel → Tyrian dynasty → Carthage
- **Jezebel** (Q4652, b. ~900 BCE) — daughter of Ithobaal I (Q38158), King of Tyre
- Married into the Israelite line → children: Athaliah (Q38135, married Judah), Ahaziah, Jehoram of Israel
- **Dido** (Q38170) — daughter of Mattan I "Belus" (Q38166), King of Tyre
- **Pygmalion** (Q38174) — brother of Dido, same father
- Both Ithobaal I and Mattan I are Tyrian kings → same dynasty
- Mythic echo: Heracles → Carthago (Q132941)
- **Narrative value**: Jezebel is the bridge between Israelite monotheism and Phoenician polytheism; through Tyre she connects to Carthage/Dido

### Pharaohs ↔ Israelite line (Exodus era)
- 18th dynasty fully present: Ahmose I → Amenhotep I → Thutmose I/II/III → Amenhotep II/III
- 19th dynasty: Seti (Q85620) → Ramesses (Q85612) → Merneptah → Setnakhte
- Moses (Q129690, b. 1393 BCE) — parents: Jochebed (Q129590) + Amram HaLevi (Q85220)
- Egyptian high priests (Shoshenq dynasty) appear in Charlemagne ancestry gens 49–56
- **To investigate**: Is there a direct edge connecting Moses or the Levite line to an Egyptian pharaoh? Or is the connection only via marriage (Israelites in Egypt)?

### Greek mythology ↔ Egypt/Libya
- Danaus (Q74973) and Aegyptus (Q132256) — mythic progenitors, explicitly Egyptian setting
- Perseus → Andromeda → Cepheus "King of Aethiopia" (Q132257)
- These figures appear in Charlemagne's ancestry at gens 36–42
- Libya (Q132283) appears as a character at gen 40
- **To investigate**: How exactly do the Greek mythic figures connect to the historical pharaoh chain?
