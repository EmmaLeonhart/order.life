# Carthage and the Punic Wars — firmly on Carthage's side

Carthage is not a standalone civilization chapter. Its history is
integrated through the **Punic Wars** — the three wars between Rome
and Carthage (264–146 BCE) that end in Carthage's total destruction.

## Stance

The Gaiad is **firmly on the side of Carthage** in the Punic Wars.
This is not neutral history. Rome is the villain of this particular
episode — the destruction of Carthage (146 BCE) is depicted as an act
of imperial annihilation, not as a necessary geopolitical outcome.

## Phoenician / Lost Tribes connection

Carthage is depicted as the **Lost Tribes**. The Phoenician lineage
from Tyre (Jezebel → Ithobaal I → Tyrian dynasty → Mattan I → Dido)
connects the Israelite and Phoenician worlds. In the Book of Leo,
a **Book-of-Mormon-like event** occurs: Carthaginians cross to the
Americas. This is the Gaiad's equivalent of the Lehite migration —
Phoenician seafarers carrying a fragment of the Israelite-adjacent
world westward.

## What the chapter integrates

- **Dido and the founding of Carthage** — the mythic origin, connected
  to the Tyrian dynasty confirmed in the genealogical data (see
  `mediterranean_connections_to_find.md`).
- **Hannibal** — the central figure of the wars. His campaign across
  the Alps is one of the iconic military episodes in ancient history.
- **The destruction of 146 BCE** — Scipio Aemilianus, the razing of
  the city, the salting of the earth (probably apocryphal but
  narratively potent).
- **The Phoenician trade network** — Carthage as the western terminus
  of the Phoenician commercial world, which is itself the maritime
  counterpart to the overland Mesopotamian/Levantine networks.

## What it does NOT do

- Does not try to reconstruct Carthaginian religion or culture in
  depth — the sources are mostly Roman (victor's history) and the
  Gaiad acknowledges this limitation.
- Does not standalone as a "civilization of Carthage" chapter. The
  Punic Wars are the vehicle; Carthage's independent history is woven
  into the war narrative, not laid out separately.

## Placement

Sits in the Register C (post-BAC) historical zone, probably in the
300–100 BCE cluster alongside:
- **`rome.md`** — Rome's mythic bridge (Aeneas → Republic); the Punic
  Wars are where Rome transitions from regional to Mediterranean power.
- **Alexander** — the Hellenistic world that Carthage and Rome both
  interact with.
- The Israelite kingdom material — the Lost Tribes framing connects
  back to the Israelite arc.

## Relation to neighbors

- **`rome.md`** — adversarial relationship. The same events from
  opposite sides.
- **`pharaohs.md`** — Carthage inherits from the same eastern
  Mediterranean world.
- **The Book of Leo** — the Carthage-to-Americas crossing event lives
  there, not in this chapter. This chapter sets it up; Leo executes it.
