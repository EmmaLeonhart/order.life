# Jimmu — messy coalition-builder, not divine founder

Shinto treatment more broadly lives in `../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md`
§7; this file is the per-figure vision for the Jimmu chapter.

## The frame

Jimmu (first Emperor of Japan) is framed **NOT as a divinely-pure
founder** but as a figure who:

- Makes **very large compromises** to achieve his goals.
- Forms an **extremely messy political coalition** between multiple
  different ethnic groups.

Subverts the divine-descent-from-Amaterasu founding narrative by
foregrounding the political-pragmatic reality. The Amaterasu lineage
stays in the picture — it's not erased — but it is not what the chapter
is *about*.

## Connecting threads

- Japan is one of the **strongest late-mythic-cycle cultures** (tier 2
  with Scandinavia — see `structure.md` §6 tiering). Jimmu sits at the
  mythic-to-historical bridge.
- The Kojiki / Nihon Shoki are the benchmark sources for how a culture
  catches its own heroic cycle at the moment of transition.
- Princess Heo Hwang-ok (Ayodhya → Korea → Japan, ~48 CE) is the one
  named Asian-to-Asian bridge figure and may anchor a companion
  chapter — see `structure.md` on the Asian frame.
