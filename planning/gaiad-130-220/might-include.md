# Might-include — candidate list for this gap

Companion to `must-include.md`. Where that file is the accountability
list (drops out = we notice), this is the **open-candidate** list:
things we *might* cover if they earn the chapter cost, but are not
committed to. Absence here doesn't mean definitely-not; presence
doesn't mean definitely-yes.

## Southeast Asia candidates

All four of these are in the might-include bucket. See
`southeast-asia.md` for the longer write-ups. Default is defer to
modern SE Asia coverage.

- ~~**Wali Sanga (Nine Saints of Java).**~~ **Promoted to
  must-include** — now a required component of the consolidated
  "Islam in Asia" chapter. See `islam-syncretic.md` and
  `must-include.md`.
- **Angkor / Jayavarman VII / the Bayon faces.** Mahayana imperial
  project at its peak and its collapse. Visually iconic; narratively
  thin without a bridge text.
- **Srivijaya.** Maritime Mahayana hub. Probably a waypoint in the
  Asia arc rather than its own chapter.
- **Ramakien / SE Asian Ramayana adaptations.** More likely a
  footnote to Indian Ramayana coverage than its own chapter.

## Other possible might-includes

Seeded here so it's a live list — additions welcome.

- **Pagan (Bagan) kingdom.** Burmese Theravada parallel to Angkor.
  Weaker case; probably skipped.
- **Romance of the Three Kingdoms.** Flagged in `asia-arc.md` as an
  open candidate. Could earn a chapter or get woven into `china.md`.
- **Tendai (Saichō) and Shingon (Kūkai).** Flagged in `asia-arc.md`
  as **significant real things** the Gaiad needs to cover, leaning
  toward must-include but not yet firm. Kūkai especially carries
  weight given the Shinto-lineage framing.
- **Empress Jingū.** Flagged in `jimmu.md` and `japan-emphasis.md`
  as an open handle for additional Japanese weight.
- **Specific Sufi figures** (Rumi, al-Ghazali, Ibn Arabi). Flagged
  in `islam-syncretic.md`. Sufism broadly is must-include; specific
  biographies are might-include.

## Promotion / demotion rules

- Moving something from might-include to must-include: add an
  entry in `must-include.md` with a pointer to its per-topic file,
  and remove from here.
- Moving something from must-include to might-include: requires a
  reason, logged in both files.
- Dropping entirely: log in the "Deliberate drops" section of
  `must-include.md` with the reason.

## Why this file exists

So that "we aren't sure if any of these really count" has a *place to
live* separate from the commitment list. Keeps `must-include.md`
clean — that file is what the gap actually owes the reader; this one
is what the writing desk has to choose from.
