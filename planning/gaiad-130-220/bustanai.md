# Bustanai — the Jewish-Persian-Islamic triple bridge

Bustanai ben Haninai (~610–670 CE), Exilarch of the Jews of Babylon
under early Islamic rule, married to **Dara**, a Sasanian princess
from the captured royal family of Yazdegerd III.

## Why he matters structurally

- **Triple bridge.** Through Bustanai × Dara, the Davidic line (Jewish
  Exilarchate), the Sasanian royal house, and — via subsequent
  generations — major Islamic-era lineages converge on a single
  genealogical node. He is the Charlemagne-analogue for the
  Jewish/Muslim/Persian side of the West Eurasian super-network.
- **Gateway ancestor** for an enormous share of descendants across three
  religious traditions that later define themselves against each other.
- Ties into the broader West Eurasian super-network: Rome + Greece/Sparta
  + Egypt + Mesopotamia + biblical + Muhammad-via-Rome + Jesus-via-Rome
  + Persian-royal-through-Bustanai.

## Narrative handling

- The Dara marriage itself is striking: a prisoner-princess from the
  fallen empire marrying the leader of a subject religious community.
  Story material as well as structural data.
- Plays against the clean "Jewish / Persian / Islamic" separation —
  the genealogy forces all three into one room.
