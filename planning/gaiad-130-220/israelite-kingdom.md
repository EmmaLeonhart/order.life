# The Israelite Kingdom — rise and fall

A chapter on the political history of the Israelite / Judahite kingdoms.
Separate from David as a person (`david.md`) and from the Torah as
doctrine (`torah.md`) — this is about the **state** and its destruction.

## What the chapter covers

- **United Monarchy** — David and Solomon's kingdom as a political
  entity. The building of the Temple. The peak.
- **The Split** — Rehoboam and Jeroboam. The northern kingdom (Israel)
  and the southern kingdom (Judah) as separate polities.
- **The polytheistic monarchy** — the Gaiad does NOT depict the
  Israelite kings as monotheists. The kingdom is **polytheistic**. The
  Omride dynasty (Ahab, Jezebel) represents normal ancient Near Eastern
  royal religion. Jezebel's Tyrian Baal worship is not an aberration —
  it's the default that the later prophetic movement reacts against.
- **Fall of Israel** (722 BCE) — Assyrian conquest, the ten lost
  tribes (connects to `carthage-punic-wars.md` via the Phoenician /
  Lost Tribes framing).
- **Fall of Judah** (586 BCE) — Babylonian conquest, destruction of
  the First Temple.

## Narrative function

This chapter sets up the prophetic movement (`prophetic-movement.md`)
by establishing what the prophets are reacting **against**: a normal,
polytheistic, politically messy ancient kingdom. Without this chapter,
the prophets have nothing to push against. The rise and fall is the
context; the prophets are the response.
