# Manu (Indian) — flood-ancestor and lawgiver

Companion to `manu-yemo.md`. Where that file handles Manu as the
Proto-Indo-European founding figure carrying the R1a Y-lineage, this
file handles the specifically **Indian** Manu — the one who matters
within Hindu tradition on its own terms, not just as a reflex of
the PIE substrate.

Both treatments are load-bearing. The PIE Manu feeds the haplogroup-
register spine; the Indian Manu is a texture-rich figure inside the
Hindu arc and needs his own coverage.

## The Indian Manu

Two main strands in Hindu tradition, not always coherently unified:

### Manu as flood-ancestor (the Matsya narrative)

- In the *Shatapatha Brahmana* and later the *Matsya Purana*: Vishnu
  in his fish (Matsya) avatar warns Manu of a coming deluge, tells him
  to build a boat and load it with seeds and sages.
- Manu survives the flood tied to the fish avatar's horn; when the
  waters recede, he is the sole progenitor of the new humanity.
- **Structural parallel with Noah** (see `genesis-opening.md`),
  Atrahasis, Utnapishtim, Deucalion — flood-survivor founding figure.
  The Gaiad doesn't need these all to be *the same story*, but the
  parallel is worth naming: the flood-ancestor motif is one of the
  most widely distributed mythic templates, and Indian tradition's
  version has Manu in the slot.
- Noah in the Gaiad's frame is the out-of-Africa migration leader
  (see `genesis-opening.md`); Manu plays the *cosmological-reset*
  version of the role within Indian tradition without the Gaiad
  committing to an actual flood.

### Manu as lawgiver (the Manusmriti)

- The *Manusmriti* (Laws of Manu) — the foundational legal / social
  / ritual code of classical Hindu tradition, compiled c. 200 BCE –
  200 CE, attributed to Manu.
- Codifies varna (the four-class system), gendered duties,
  purity/pollution rules, life-stage obligations. Its influence on
  post-Vedic Hindu society is enormous.
- The Gaiad's frame: this is the *Indian* codification moment that
  parallels what the Torah does for Jewish tradition — taking an
  inherited mythic-ancestor figure and attaching a legal-ritual corpus
  to him. Manu-as-lawgiver is doing the same structural work as
  Moses-as-lawgiver, even though the content and historical context
  differ entirely.

## Why this parallelism matters to the Gaiad

- **Lawgiver-attribution as a civilizational move.** Moses, Manu,
  Confucius (in a different key), Solon — the move of anchoring a
  legal tradition to a named foundational figure is a pattern the
  Gaiad notices. Manu is the Indian instance.
- **Flood-ancestor as the shared substrate.** The Manu/Noah parallel
  doesn't require a single actual flood. The Gaiad can hold "these
  are independent reflexes of a very deep motif about
  civilizational-reset-after-catastrophe" without flattening them.
- **Indian-Hindu tradition on its own terms.** Keeping Manu as an
  Indian-interior figure (not just a PIE reflex) gives the Hindu
  material its own weight in the Asia arc (see `asia-arc.md`).
  Hindu tradition isn't a relay for Indo-European substrate — it
  does its own structural work with the inherited materials.
