# Cucuteni-Trypillia — old Europe before Yamnaya

One of the required scale-correction inclusions (see `structure.md`
and `CLAUDE.md`). The enormous Neolithic / Copper Age culture of
eastern Europe (~5500–2750 BCE), famous for painted pottery, planned
mega-settlements, and a long run of apparent egalitarian
matrifocal organization — all erased by the Yamnaya / Indo-European
expansion that brought in Manu and Yemo (see `manu-yemo.md`).

## Why it matters

- **Scale.** Some Cucuteni-Trypillia settlements were among the largest
  human habitations anywhere at their time — tens of thousands of
  residents, planned street grids, cyclic deliberate burning and
  rebuilding. Not a footnote culture.
- **Pre-IE Europe.** The world the R1a/R1b steppe migration arrives
  into, and overwrites.
- **No script, no native voice.** Like `indus-valley.md`, we are reading
  a large civilization entirely from the outside, through archaeology.
  The mythic-cycle mechanism in `structure.md` §6 does not apply.

## What the chapter can do

- Establish **what the Indo-Europeans replaced** — not a thin
  hunter-gatherer landscape but a mature Neolithic / Copper Age world
  with its own coherence.
- The settlement-burning pattern, the painted pottery, the apparent
  absence of militarized hierarchy → texture. Let the material register
  the fact that a complete civilizational tradition passed out of the
  record.
- Pairs thematically with `lithuania-counterfactual.md` — another case
  of an eastern-European tradition foreclosed before it could write
  itself into legibility, though for very different reasons and at very
  different scales.

## Placement

Register A. Slightly before or overlapping with the Yamnaya expansion
and Manu / Yemo material. Part of the opening cluster of haplogroup-
era chapters, not a standalone late chapter.
