# Manu and Yemo — the Proto-Indo-European founding pair (R1a / R1b)

Emma, explicit: **Manu and Yemo are R1a and R1b.** The Proto-Indo-
European cosmogonic twin myth is carried by the Gaiad as the
*in-narrative* identity of the two great western-Eurasian Y-lineages.

## The myth

Reconstructed PIE founding pair. Manu (*"man"*) and Yemo (*"twin"*) —
the first man and his twin. In the foundational version, Yemo is
sacrificed by Manu to create the world's order: body parts become the
cosmos, social classes, livestock. Reflexes across the daughter
traditions:

- **Germanic** — Mannus and Ymir.
- **Indic** — Manu and Yama.
- **Roman** — Romulus and Remus (fratricide preserved; cosmogonic
  payoff attenuated).
- **Iranian** — Gayomart / Mashya / Mashyana cluster.

## The identification

In the Gaiad's frame, this is **not** a symbolic mapping. The PIE twin
pair *is* the mythic register of the two Y-haplogroup populations:

- **Manu = R1a** — the Indo-Iranian / Balto-Slavic branch carrier.
- **Yemo = R1b** — the western-Celto-Italic / Germanic branch carrier.

(Or the reverse, depending on how the R1a/R1b association shakes out
under the genealogy dump. The point is the *identification*, not the
specific assignment.)

## What this lets the Gaiad do

- The **Yamnaya / Indo-European expansion** — the ~3000 BCE steppe
  migration that carried R1a and R1b across Eurasia — has a ready
  mythic vocabulary built in. It is not a dry archaeological chapter;
  it is Manu and Yemo entering Europe and India.
- The myth's **daughter reflexes** (Ymir, Yama, Remus, Gayomart) become
  legible as preserved pieces of one story across the descendant
  cultures — the same move the Gaiad makes in `greece.md` for
  Dionysus/Prometheus/Heracles, but with a clearer genealogical basis
  because the haplogroups are literal.
- Required scale-correction material (Indo-European / Yamnaya — see
  `structure.md`) gets its protagonist.
