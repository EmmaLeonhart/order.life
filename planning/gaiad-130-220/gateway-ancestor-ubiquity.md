# Gateway ancestors — ubiquity is the point

Cross-cutting note. The Gaiad's treatment of genealogical-spine figures
(see `structure.md` §7) rests on a specific premise about how descent
actually works over long timescales: **the gateway ancestors are
gateways precisely because descent from them is ubiquitous**, not
because it is rare or secret.

## The math

Human pedigree collapse is brutal. Go back ~30–50 generations and, for
any large interconnected population, you reach an **Identical Ancestors
Point** — a date before which every living person of that population
is descended from every then-living person who has any descendants at
all. Charlemagne, Muhammad, Bustanai, Genghis, probably Jesus — every
such figure with downstream descendants is an ancestor of nearly
everyone in the relevant downstream population, many times over.

This is not a Da Vinci Code secret. It is the default fact about
genealogy once you let enough time elapse.

## Why the Gaiad leans into this rather than around it

- **Honesty.** The alternative — secret bloodlines, concealed
  succession, royal-blood-as-rare-privilege — is a Victorian-era
  distortion that genuine genealogy does not support. The Gaiad does
  not pretend otherwise.
- **Tone fit.** Ubiquity-made-visible is a cleaner X-Files move than
  rare-secret-revealed. You don't expect the emperor's Jesus descent
  to be *trivially shared* with the peasant whose descent he doesn't
  know; once you see it, the structural irony is sharper than any
  conspiracy reveal.
- **Super-network coherence.** The West Eurasian super-network (Rome +
  Greece/Sparta + Egypt + Mesopotamia + biblical + Muhammad-via-Rome +
  Jesus-via-Rome + Persian-royal-through-Bustanai) is legible only if
  you accept that these lines fan out to nearly everyone in the
  downstream populations. The Gaiad's project depends on this.

## Where the ubiquity shows up in the per-figure files

- `charlemagne.md` — European populations, descent from Charlemagne is
  nearly universal.
- `bustanai.md` — Jewish, Persian, Islamic lines converge downstream.
- `mongolia.md` — Genghis's Y-DNA as one of the most widespread modern
  signatures.
- `jesus.md` — Jesus-via-Rome descent is a real claim with the same
  ubiquity behavior. See especially `marozia.md` for the Pornocracy /
  papal-descent open question.
- `buddha.md` — Rāhula / Shakya descent is an **open question** in the
  same ubiquity frame.

## The handling question each chapter has to solve

Because ubiquity is the default, the per-figure chapters can't do their
work just by announcing "X is your ancestor." They have to decide what
the *specific* visibility move is:

1. **Traceability** — the figure is a gateway not because the descent
   is unique, but because the **records** from them onward are
   unusually complete.
2. **Concentration** — specific families preserved or re-concentrated
   the descent in a legible way (Marozia, Solomonic dynasty).
3. **Literal-irony beat** — the descent is ubiquitous AND landed
   somewhere structurally funny (Jesus descendants running the
   medieval papacy; Rāhula descendants continuing despite being named
   "obstacle"). Note the nuance captured in `marozia.md`: the
   House of Theophylact doesn't just share the ubiquitous line — they
   carry a denser, more traceable concentration of it, *and* this
   concentration is genealogically arbitrary. Both facts are
   load-bearing. The concentration is real; its arbitrariness is the
   Gaiad's actual point.
4. **Cross-cultural bridge** — the descent connects groups that later
   define themselves *against* each other (Bustanai's Jewish / Persian
   / Islamic triple node).

Each figure's chapter picks one or more of these. Open for several —
see marozia.md, buddha.md.

## Open meta-question

Across the book, should the ubiquity itself get a **dedicated chapter**
where the Identical-Ancestors-Point math is made explicit, or should
it stay as an ambient premise that the gateway-ancestor chapters
illustrate without ever stating directly? Both are viable. Currently
unresolved.
