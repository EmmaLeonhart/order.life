# Armenia — hard requirement

**Hard requirement.** Armenia cannot be skipped or deprioritized.

## Why it's hard

- **First Christian kingdom** (traditionally 301 CE under Tiridates III
  / Gregory the Illuminator). This is structurally significant in the
  monotheism framework — Armenia adopted Christianity before Rome did,
  making it the earliest state-level Christian commitment.
- **Movses Khorenatsi** (5th c.) — the *History of the Armenians*
  traces from **Haik** (mythic ancestor) through the Arsacid dynasty.
  This is a clean mythic-to-historical bridge in the Caucasian frame.
- **Genocide resonance** — Armenia has a living contemporary identity
  shaped by survival. Like Egypt and Ethiopia, the ancient material
  connects to something modern audiences know and care about.
- **Caucasian cluster** — Armenia pairs naturally with Georgia
  (*Life of Kartli*, Bagrationi dynasty). The two together form a
  Caucasian sub-cluster that connects into the broader super-network
  through the Arsacid / Parthian royal connections.

## What the chapter does

- Haik as mythic founder → Arsacid dynasty → conversion to
  Christianity → the Armenian identity that persists through Byzantine,
  Persian, Arab, Ottoman, and Russian domination.
- The **conversion** is the key structural beat: Armenia converting
  before Constantine means the deflationary-polytheism frame has to
  account for a Christian state that predates the Sol Invictus /
  Constantine synthesis. This is a genuine complication, not just a
  footnote.
- Connects to Ethiopia as a fellow early-Christian-kingdom with a
  mythic genealogical claim (Solomonic for Ethiopia, Haik for Armenia).

## Coverage scope

One chapter, possibly shared with Georgia if the Caucasian cluster
compresses well. The Haik material is short; the Arsacid/Parthian
connection and the conversion are the load-bearing beats.

## Relation to neighbors

- **`ethiopia.md`** — parallel structure (early Christian kingdom with
  mythic genealogy).
- **`persia.md`** — the Arsacid dynasty is Parthian; Armenia and
  Persia share deep royal-genealogical connections.
- **`byzantium.md`** — Armenia as a Byzantine frontier zone and
  cultural bridge.
- **`jesus.md`** / Sol Invictus material — Armenia's earlier conversion
  complicates the "Christianity = Sol Invictus continuation" claim and
  needs to be addressed.
