# The Prophetic Movement — the minor prophets as extremist founders

**Structural decision (firm).** The major and minor prophets are
depicted as the **actual founders of Yahwist Judaism** — an
ideologically extreme group that opposed the polytheistic Jewish
monarchy. They are not treated as divinely inspired reformers calling
Israel back to its true faith. They are treated as radicals who
**invented** strict monotheism and then imposed it.

## The framing

The prophets are basically **Hamas**. An extremist faction arising from
within a normal political society, driven by ideological absolutism,
eventually gaining enough power to reshape the entire culture.

- The polytheistic Israelite monarchy (`israelite-kingdom.md`) is the
  establishment they're fighting.
- The prophetic literature (Amos, Hosea, Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel,
  the Twelve) is their propaganda and their theological program.
- Their victory is not military but **editorial** — they or their
  heirs control what gets included in the biblical canon, so the
  historical record is written from their perspective.

## Chapter sequence

This chapter follows the Israelite kingdom chapter and flows into the

   that rules over people."
4. **`second-temple.md`** — the return and the Second Temple period as
   its own thing.

## What the prophets actually did

- **Invented strict monotheism** as a political-theological program,
  not received it as revelation.
- **Opposed the polytheistic monarchy** — Elijah vs. Ahab/Jezebel is
  the iconic confrontation, but it's one episode in a longer pattern.
- **Used the exile to consolidate** — with the monarchy destroyed and
  the population displaced, the prophetic/priestly faction became the
  only organized leadership. The exile is their opportunity.
- **Controlled the canon** — the editorial history of the Hebrew Bible
  reflects prophetic priorities. The Deuteronomistic History (Joshua
  through Kings) judges every king by whether they were monotheistic
  enough — that's the prophetic faction's scorecard, retroactively
  applied.

## Tone

Deflationary but not dismissive. The prophets are portrayed as
extremists, but **effective** extremists whose program succeeded so
thoroughly that it reshaped three world religions. The Gaiad respects
the achievement while refusing to accept the prophets' own framing of
themselves as righteous reformers.
