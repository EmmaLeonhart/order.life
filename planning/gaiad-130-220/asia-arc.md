# The Asia arc вЂ” shape, skew, and what Japan climaxes

The Gaiad's Asia coverage is **not** a balanced survey of Asian
civilizations. It has a deliberate shape, and that shape climaxes in
Japan. This file names the shape so the per-topic files (`china.md`,
`xuanzang.md`, `jimmu.md`, `japan-emphasis.md`, etc.) don't each
reinvent the framing.

## The spine

The Asia arc is structured as a **single flowing movement**:

1. **Aryan invasion / Indo-European entry into South Asia** вЂ” the
   origin of Hinduism. (Connects back to `manu-yemo.md` вЂ” R1a, Yamnaya,
   the PIE expansion going east as well as west.)
2. **Hinduism** вЂ” Ramayana, Mahabharata. The great epics as the core
   texture.
3. **Buddhism emerges from Hinduism** вЂ” the Buddha chapter
   (`buddha.md`) is the pivot.
4. **Mahayana emerges from Buddhism** вЂ” the doctrinal shift that makes
   Buddhism exportable eastward.
5. **Mahayana moves into China** вЂ” with the Manichaean corridor running
   alongside (see `buddha.md` for the RДЃhula-bloodline-goes-east move).
6. **Journey to the West** вЂ” `xuanzang.md`. The Asian-side bridge
   chapter.
7. **Buddhism moves into Japan** вЂ” Tendai, Shingon. KЕ«kai and SaichЕЌ.
   The religion's eastward migration reaches its terminal shore.

This arc is the *primary* Asia storyline. Other Asian threads exist
but don't drive the main movement.

## Why Japan climaxes

- The Kojiki-of-humanity framing (see `japan-emphasis.md`) already
  tilts the weighting toward Japan.
- The Buddhism-moves-east arc *structurally* climaxes in Japan вЂ” it's
  the last major landmass east of the Buddhist origin before the
  Pacific. Tendai and Shingon are the late-stage crystallizations.
- Japanese independent philosophical / scientific development
  (post-Heian, Edo, etc.) also moves through Japan rather than being
  framed as peripheral. The arc treats Japan as *where things land*,
  not as an outpost.

## China and India вЂ” covered, not at equal intensity

China and India get real coverage (`china.md`, `xuanzang.md`,
`buddha.md`, `manu-yemo.md`, `indus-valley.md`), but not at the same
intensity as the Aryanв†’Hinduismв†’Buddhismв†’Mahayanaв†’Chinaв†’Japan spine.

- **China** has Shiji, Romance of the Three Kingdoms candidate
  material, independent philosophical development (Confucianism вЂ”
  see below), but these are woven *through* the arc rather than
  given their own parallel-equal-weight spine.
- **India** gets the Ramayana/Mahabharata and the Buddha's origin,
  but Indian history after Buddhism's eastward departure thins out.
  The `founder-followership-displacement.md` dynamic (Buddhism leaving
  India) reinforces this вЂ” the Gaiad's Buddhist arc *follows the
  religion east* rather than staying to cover India's Hindu
  consolidation in equal depth.

Not a slight. A consequence of the arc's shape.

## Southeast Asia вЂ” low-intensity by default

Southeast Asia is **not covered with much intensity** unless a
particularly good mythic cycle or episode surfaces. The default is:
skip or near-skip.

- Angkor, Srivijaya, Majapahit, Pagan, etc. вЂ” real civilizations,
  real mythic material вЂ” but the Gaiad isn't obligated to cover
  everything, and chapter scarcity is real.
- If something surfaces that earns its chapter (a late-mythic-cycle
  bridge comparable to `aztec.md`, `scandinavia.md`, `jimmu.md`),
  it gets its own file. Otherwise, pass.
- Indonesia's Islamic trajectory interacts with
  `founder-followership-displacement.md` (Islam's primary demographic
  weight shifting to non-Arab populations) вЂ” that may pull a small
  amount of Southeast Asian material into the `muhammad.md` /
  post-Muhammad Islam thread, but not as a standalone Southeast
  Asia arc.

## Guardrail

This is about arc *shape*, not per-culture worth. The Gaiad isn't
claiming Japan is more important than India or China. It's claiming
the arc it's telling вЂ” one specific arc, out of many possible ones вЂ”
happens to climax there. Other arcs could be told; this one is ours.
