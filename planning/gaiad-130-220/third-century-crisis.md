# Third Century Crisis and Sol Invictus — Christianity from paganism

**Structural decision.** The Sol Invictus material and the rise of
Christianity as Rome practiced it are covered in the **same chapter**
as the Third Century Crisis (235–284 CE). This is not a separate
"Christianity chapter" — it's a Roman political-religious chapter
where Christianity emerges as one outcome of a collapsing imperial
religious landscape.

## The argument

Christianity as Rome practiced it comes **directly out of Sol
Invictus**. The deflationary-polytheism frame treats this as
continuous, not as a rupture:

- The Third Century Crisis destabilizes Roman religion along with
  everything else.
- **Sol Invictus** (the Unconquered Sun) becomes the dominant late-
  Roman cult — Aurelian makes it the official cult in 274 CE.
- **Constantine** adopts Christianity, but the Christianity he adopts
  is deeply shaped by the solar-cult landscape. Christmas on December
  25 (Sol Invictus's birthday), the halo iconography, the Sunday
  sabbath, the imperial-cult structure of the early Church.
- The Charlemagne ancestry document (`charlemagne_to_adam.md`) makes
  this concrete: trace Charlemagne's immediate ancestors backward and
  you see the pagan-to-Christian transition **generation by generation**.
  There is no clean break. The genealogical super-network shows
  continuity where the tradition claims conversion.

## What the chapter covers

- **The Crisis itself** — the 50-year period of near-collapse. Barracks
  emperors, plague, fragmentation (Gallic Empire, Palmyrene Empire).
- **Aurelian and Sol Invictus** — the reunification of the empire under
  a solar-cult umbrella.
- **Diocletian's reforms** — tetrarchy, the last major persecution of
  Christians.
- **Constantine's conversion** — presented as a political-religious
  move continuous with Sol Invictus, not as a break from paganism.
- The **Edict of Milan** (313) and **Edict of Thessalonica** (380) —
  Christianity becoming the state religion by the same mechanisms that
  Sol Invictus became one.

## Relation to `elagabalus.md`

The Elagabalus material (black stone of Emesa → Kaaba connection) is
**adjacent but distinct**. Elagabalus is the X-Files connective
episode; this chapter is the broader political-religious context.
Elagabalus (r. 218–222) is chronologically just before the Crisis
begins (235). The two could be in the same chapter or adjacent
chapters — the Emesa solar cult feeds into Sol Invictus feeds into
Constantine.
