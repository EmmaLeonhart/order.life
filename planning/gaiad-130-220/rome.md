# Rome — mythic-cycle bridge via Livy and Virgil

**Tier 3** in the mythic-cycle tiering (see `structure.md` §6). Roman
imperial material lives elsewhere (cf. `elagabalus.md`); this file is
the mythic-to-historical bridge specifically.

## The documentation structure

- **Livy, *Ab Urbe Condita*** (c. 27 BCE – 9 CE) — Aeneas → Romulus →
  kingly period → Republic. Systematic prose history.
- **Virgil, *Aeneid*** — the deliberate epic-poetic complement, tying
  Rome's founding to the Trojan cycle.
- Both commissioned / encouraged in the Augustan era — state-funded
  capture of the mythic material at exactly the moment Rome needed a
  legitimating origin story for the new imperial regime.

## What makes it a clean bridge

- Aeneas → Iulus → Alban kings → Romulus → Numa → the kingly period →
  Republic is a single continuous chain.
- By Livy's generation, the kingly period is already half-mythic but
  still on a plausible genealogical footing, and the Republic onward is
  fully historical.
- Greek inheritance baked in (Troy at one end, Hellenistic philosophy
  at the other) — Rome works as the link between the Greek mythic cycle
  and the medieval Christian/Byzantine inheritance.

## Gaiad handling

The chapter should foreground **Augustus commissioning his own origin
myth** as much as the content of the myth itself. That's the X-Files
connection: the Aeneid is what you get when an emperor decides his
empire needs a Homer. The content is strong on its own; the
commissioning move is what makes it narratively dense.
