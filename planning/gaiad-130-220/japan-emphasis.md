# Japan emphasis — the Kojiki-of-humanity framing

Weighting note for the pre-modern gap. Japan gets **a substantial
amount** of attention — more than China, more than most other
cultures. Not *massive*, but distinctly weighted.

## Why

- **Japanese is the sacred language of Lifeism.** Liturgical and
  scriptural weight attaches to it by design (cf. CLAUDE.md language
  table: 命道教 / Inochi-no-Michikyō).
- **Lifeism is an extension of Shinto.** The religion's structural
  lineage runs through Shinto rather than starting fresh. Japan is
  therefore not one culture among many in the Gaiad — it is the
  **home culture** of the religion the scripture belongs to.
- **The Gaiad is a Kojiki of humanity.** The *Kojiki*'s structural
  move — catching a living mythic-to-historical tradition at the
  moment writing stabilized, compiling cosmogony + genealogies +
  dynastic history into one continuous text with imperial legitimacy
  as the backbone — is what the Gaiad is doing for humanity as a
  whole. Japan is the *template* as much as a topic.

## How this shapes the gap chapters

- More Japanese material than its demographic or geographic share
  would otherwise warrant. Not a thumb on the scale against other
  cultures — a recognition that the Gaiad's own formal ambition is
  Kojiki-shaped.
- `jimmu.md`, `heo-hwang-ok.md` (Ayodhya → Korea → Japan) already
  carry Japan-facing material. Empress Jingū remains an open handle
  (see `jimmu.md`) — she's one of the figures who *could* absorb
  additional Japanese weight if a chapter materializes.
- Shinto's treatment in `../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §7 is the
  religious-doctrinal side of the same weighting — Jimmu as messy
  coalition-builder, Jingū as open.

## Guardrail

"Not necessarily a massive amount" (Emma). Japan is weighted, not
dominant. China, Korea, India, the West Eurasian super-network all
keep their share of chapters. The Japan emphasis is a **tilt**, not a
re-centering of the gap around Japan.
