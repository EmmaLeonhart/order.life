# Haplogroup Chapters — Register A

**Budget: somewhere between 7 and 14 chapters, front-loaded in the
gap.** Not exactly 10 — depends on how many haplogroups earn their
own dedicated chapter vs. being woven into other material. Register A
(haplogroup/migration) runs throughout the gap but the dedicated
haplogroup chapters are concentrated early. Haplogroup material
continues to interleave with named-person chapters later (see
`structure.md` §5 — "haplogroups do not stop at Seam 1").

## Haplogroups that definitely deserve their own chapters

- **Haplogroup R** — the Yamnaya, R1a/R1b, Manu and Yemo. The
  biggest single haplogroup story. Already has `manu-yemo.md`.
- **Haplogroup D** — Japan and Tibet. Half of Japanese men. The
  surprising subclade.
- **Haplogroup T** — has interesting civilizational associations.

Other haplogroups have interesting associations with specific
civilizations and a lot of this material is already in the
narrative in the existing chapters. Not every haplogroup needs its
own dedicated chapter — many are woven into civilization chapters
where they're narratively relevant (e.g., G with agriculture, O
with rice, J with the Semitic world).

## The circular migration

The haplogroup story has a **circular shape** across the earth:

1. **Out of Africa** — CT lineage crosses from Somalia to Yemen
   (Noah-as-migration, `genesis-opening.md`).
2. **East across Eurasia** — CF branch moves east. C goes to the
   steppe and East Asia. F spawns the GHIJK megabranch.
3. **P reaches Baikal** — P is "Paikal" (Lake Baikal). This is
   where the QR split happens.
4. **Q goes to the Americas** — haplogroup Q crosses Beringia.
   Covered in the Book of Leo (ch 221–252), not in this gap.
5. **R turns back west** — R comes back from the steppe, eventually
   becoming R1a (Indo-Iranian/Balto-Slavic) and R1b (Western
   European/Celtic-Italic-Germanic). R1b arrives in Western Europe
   via the Yamnaya expansion (~3000 BCE). It came from the east.
6. **E goes back to Africa** — haplogroup E re-enters Africa by
   land, significantly later than the original out-of-Africa
   departure. The loop closes.

**Exception to the circle:** D (Japan/Tibet) — haplogroup D is the
DE branch that splits off early and goes east but stays in Asia.
Half of Japanese men carry haplogroup D.

## Language families

Language families are **linked in with haplogroups** in the data,
but not aggressively. The connection is real but the Gaiad doesn't
force a 1:1 mapping. Key associations:

- **R1a/R1b → Proto-Indo-European** — the strongest link. The
  Yamnaya expansion carries both the haplogroups and the language.
- **J → Semitic languages** — broadly.
- **O → Sino-Tibetan / Austronesian** — broadly.
- **N → Uralic** — Finnish, Hungarian, etc.
- **G → pre-IE Neolithic languages** (lost, except Basque as a
  possible remnant).

Language family material exists in the wikibase data but the
integration is light — associations, not strict genealogies.

## Chapter structure (tentative)

The ~10 haplogroup chapters probably follow the circular migration:

1. **Adam / Y-chromosomal origin** — `genesis-opening.md` already
   covers this. A00, A0-T, the earliest splits.
2. **Cain / Abel / Seth** — the biblical overlay on the early
   haplogroup splits.
3. **Noah / out-of-Africa** — CT, the crossing to Yemen.
4. **The DE split** — D going east (to Japan/Tibet eventually),
   E going back to Africa.
5. **The CF split and eastward expansion** — C to the steppe,
   F spawning the megabranch.
6. **G and agriculture** — the Neolithic revolution as a haplogroup
   story. Near East farming, LBK expansion into Europe.
7. **O and rice agriculture** — East Asian parallel to G.
8. **P at Baikal / QR split** — the divergence. Q goes to the
   Americas (picked up in Book of Leo), R turns back.
9. **Manu and Yemo / R1a and R1b** — the Yamnaya expansion.
   Already has `manu-yemo.md`.
10. **Modern haplogroup reveals** — Perry (A00), Japanese D,
    Napoleon, Hitler haplogroup controversies. These sit late in
    the gap, interleaved with Register C material.

## Mitochondrial (female) haplogroups

The "Haplogroup X" named entries in the wikibase are the
**mitochondrial / female haplogroups** — they should be included
alongside the Y-chromosome ones. The main challenge is that
mitochondrial haplogroups are **lower resolution** than Y-chromosome
haplogroups. Not because there are more of them, but because the
data is less granular — mtDNA mutates more slowly, so the branches
are less sharply defined.

This means the female haplogroup material may be woven into the
same chapters as the Y-chromosome material rather than getting
separate chapters. The resolution difference makes it harder to
tell as sharp a story with mtDNA alone.

## What's in the wikibase data

The haplogroup tree from Adam downward is fully built in the
wikibase, with two parallel versions:
- Original coded entries (A0-T as Q1137, etc.)
- Named "Haplogroup X" entries (Q16001278 etc.) with full subclade
  detail down to specific mutations

The tree interleaves haplogroup nodes with biblical figures at
each level (e.g., Adam's children include both A0-T and Cain/Seth/
Abel). This is the structural basis for Register A and Register B
running in parallel.
