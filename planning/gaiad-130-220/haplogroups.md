# Haplogroup Chapters — Register A

**Budget: somewhere between 7 and 14 chapters, front-loaded in the
gap.** Not exactly 10 — depends on how many haplogroups earn their
own dedicated chapter vs. being woven into other material. Register A
(haplogroup/migration) runs throughout the gap but the dedicated
haplogroup chapters are concentrated early. Haplogroup material
continues to interleave with named-person chapters later (see
`structure.md` §5 — "haplogroups do not stop at Seam 1").

## Haplogroups — they probably all deserve their own chapters

The question isn't really which haplogroups earn chapters — they
probably all do. It's a matter of how they're structured. Some
will be standalone haplogroup chapters; others might share a
chapter or be the haplogroup layer of a civilization chapter.

### A, B — staying in Africa

The haplogroups that stay in Africa (A, B) don't have a lot of
standalone narrative interest. A00 has the Perry case as a modern
reveal (late chapter material), but the deep African lineages are
mostly the origin point — the interestingness is in what moves
out. These are covered in the genesis opening chapters
(`genesis-opening.md`) rather than earning their own dedicated
haplogroup chapters.

### L, T, H — the Indus problem

Haplogroups L, T, and H all have at least some potential link to
the **Indus Valley civilization**. This makes them individually the
least interesting haplogroups for standalone chapters, because
their civilizational anchor is essentially illegible. The Indus is
in an uncanny valley of knowledge — too well-documented to freely
mythologize (like Göbekli Tepe), but too undeciphered to narrate
with confidence. Anything you write about the Indus risks being
just plausible enough to be wrong. See `indus-valley.md`.

These three probably don't get standalone haplogroup chapters.
They're more likely woven into the Indus Valley chapter itself
(which is already framed around illegibility) or into the
Indo-European arrival material where they represent the pre-existing
population the R1a migrations encounter.

### C — the unsplit haplogroup

Haplogroup C is not just "Mongol steppe." It's also the major
lineage of **Indigenous Australians** (~60% of Aboriginal men carry
C). This gives C a dual identity spanning two very different
worlds: the oldest continuous culture on earth (Australia, peopled
~50 kya) and the steppe nomads who built the Mongol Empire. One
haplogroup, two completely different stories.

**The puzzle:** C should have been split into two haplogroups by
now. The geographic and temporal distance between Indigenous
Australia and the Central Asian steppe is enormous. D got scattered
into refugia by Toba, but C's story seems different — it's not
obviously a catastrophe-and-survival pattern. There's potentially
a more continuous migration story here, but it's unclear what that
story is or to what degree C is truly clustered in just these two
places vs. having intermediate populations. The fact that genetics
hasn't split C into separate haplogroups despite this spread is
itself an open question that may shape the chapter.

Unlike D (where the chapter is about the catastrophe), C's chapter
may be about the **mystery of non-divergence** — how one lineage
spans the oldest continuous culture and the greatest steppe
conquerors without becoming two separate things.

## The circular migration

The haplogroup story has a **circular shape** across the earth:

1. **Out of Africa** — CT lineage crosses from Somalia to Yemen
   (Noah-as-migration, `genesis-opening.md`).
2. **East across Eurasia** — CF branch moves east. C goes to the
   steppe and East Asia. F spawns the GHIJK megabranch.
3. **P reaches Baikal** — P is "Paikal" (Lake Baikal). This is
   where the QR split happens.
4. **Q goes to the Americas** — haplogroup Q crosses Beringia.
   Covered in the Book of Leo (ch 221–252), not in this gap.
5. **R turns back west** — R comes back from the steppe, eventually
   becoming R1a (Indo-Iranian/Balto-Slavic) and R1b (Western
   European/Celtic-Italic-Germanic). R1b arrives in Western Europe
   via the Yamnaya expansion (~3000 BCE). It came from the east.
6. **E goes back to Africa** — haplogroup E re-enters Africa by
   land, significantly later than the original out-of-Africa
   departure. The loop closes.

**Exception to the circle:** D (Japan/Tibet) — haplogroup D is the
DE branch that splits off early and goes east but stays in Asia.
Half of Japanese men carry haplogroup D.

## Language families

Language families are **linked in with haplogroups** in the data,
but not aggressively. The connection is real but the Gaiad doesn't
force a 1:1 mapping. Key associations:

- **R1a/R1b → Proto-Indo-European** — the strongest link. The
  Yamnaya expansion carries both the haplogroups and the language.
- **J → Semitic languages** — broadly.
- **O → Sino-Tibetan / Austronesian** — broadly.
- **N → Uralic** — Finnish, Hungarian, etc.
- **G → pre-IE Neolithic languages** (lost, except Basque as a
  possible remnant).

Language family material exists in the wikibase data but the
integration is light — associations, not strict genealogies.

## Draft chapter proposal — haplogroup tree as narrative spine

The chapters follow the **Y-chromosome tree branching** as their
primary organizing principle, with civilizations and language
families woven in where a haplogroup naturally anchors them. This
is NOT a rigid sequence — there is significant fuzziness between
chapters, and some of these may merge or reorder at the writing
desk. Language families and civilizations don't map 1:1 to
haplogroups; the associations are real but loose.

### Phase 1: Origin and first splits (ch ~130–134)

**1. Adam / Y-chromosomal origin**
- Haplogroups: A, A00, A0-T
- Biblical overlay: Adam
- A and B stay in Africa — the origin point. The interestingness
  is in what moves out. A00 returns as the Perry case much later.
- See `genesis-opening.md`.

**2. Cain / Abel / Seth — the first splits**
- Haplogroups: the A vs. BT divergence
- Biblical overlay: Cain, Abel, Seth as the mythic register of the
  earliest Y-chromosome branching
- Register A and Register B running in parallel from the start —
  haplogroup nodes and biblical figures interleaved at each level
  (as built in the wikibase)

**3. Noah / out of Africa**
- Haplogroup: **CT** — the out-of-Africa carrier
- Biblical overlay: Noah's migration from Somalia across Bab
  al-Mandab to Yemen. The flood is real but local — a boat to
  cross the Red Sea, not a global deluge.
- This is the starting gun for everything else. Every non-African
  haplogroup descends from CT.
- See `genesis-opening.md`.

### Phase 2: The great divergences (ch ~135–139)

**4. The DE split / D and Toba**
- Haplogroup: **DE → D**
- D was once widespread across southern Asia. The **Toba
  catastrophe** (~74 kya) wiped it out everywhere except isolated
  refugia: **Japan, Tibet, Andaman Islands**.
- The story is catastrophe and survival in disconnected pockets,
  NOT a forced cultural connection between the surviving
  populations.
- Half of Japanese men carry D today. That's the hook for the
  Japan thread later.
- E splits off here too but its chapter comes later (the
  loop-closer).

**5. The CF split / C — the unsplit haplogroup**
- Haplogroup: **CF → C**
- C goes to **Indigenous Australia** (~60% of Aboriginal men) AND
  the **Central Asian steppe** (Mongol lineage).
- The puzzle: C should have been split into two haplogroups given
  the distance, but wasn't. Unlike D (catastrophe explains the
  scatter), C has a more continuous but unknown story.
- Dual identity chapter: oldest continuous culture on earth +
  greatest steppe conquerors.
- F spawns the GHIJK megabranch — everything from here down.

**6. The IJ split / I — old Europe's hunters**
- Haplogroup: **I**
- Pre-Indo-European European lineage. The hunter-gatherers who
  were there before the Neolithic farmers (G) and the steppe
  riders (R) arrived.
- Civilization: connects to **Cucuteni-Trypillia** and the pre-IE
  European world that the Yamnaya expansion eventually overwrites.
  See `cucuteni-trypillia.md`.
- Language: whatever pre-IE European languages existed — lost,
  with Basque as a possible distant remnant (though Basque may be
  more G-associated).

### Phase 3: The builders (ch ~140–145)

**7. G builds the Neolithic world**
- Haplogroup: **G**
- Civilization: **Göbekli Tepe**, invention of agriculture,
  invention of architecture. The haplogroup that builds
  civilization. Near East farming → LBK expansion into Europe.
- Language: pre-IE Neolithic languages of the Near East and
  Europe — mostly lost. Basque as possible remnant. Whatever
  the first farmers spoke.
- This is the chapter where humanity changes mode — from foraging
  to building. G is the genetic signature of that transition.

**8. J — the Semitic world**
- Haplogroup: **J** (J1 = Arabian Peninsula, J2 = Levantine /
  Anatolian)
- Language: **Semitic languages** → broader **Afroasiatic** family.
  Arabic, Hebrew, Aramaic, Akkadian, Amharic — all Semitic. The
  Afroasiatic family also includes Egyptian, Berber, Cushitic,
  Chadic.
- Civilization: the entire ancient Near Eastern / Middle Eastern
  world. This haplogroup anchors everything from Sumer through
  the Arabian Peninsula through Ethiopia.
- J2 specifically ties to the Levantine / Anatolian Neolithic
  farming expansion — overlaps with G but in a different
  geographic zone.

**9. H, L, T → the Indus Valley (shared chapter)**
- Haplogroups: **H, L, T** (all with Indus Valley / South Asian
  associations; possibly Dravidian or Elamite-related)
- Civilization: **Indus Valley** — Harappa, Mohenjo-daro. The
  largest pre-IE South Asian civilization. Script undeciphered.
- These are the individually least interesting haplogroups because
  their civilizational anchor is illegible. The Indus is in an
  uncanny valley: too well-documented to mythologize freely, too
  undeciphered to narrate with confidence.
- Chapter is about the **illegibility itself** as much as the
  civilization. See `indus-valley.md`.
- The Dravidian/Aryan relationship is collaborative, not
  adversarial — no "eternal war" framing.

### Phase 4: East Asia and the Pacific rim (ch ~146–149)

**10. NO split / N — the Uralic peoples**
- Haplogroup: **N**
- Language: **Uralic** — Finnish, Estonian, Hungarian, Sami,
  Samoyedic. One of the few non-IE language families that
  survived in Europe.
- The N chapter is about linguistic survival against the
  Indo-European tide. Finnish and Hungarian aren't IE languages
  because they carry a different haplogroup signature.
- Connects to the Finno-Ugric migration from Siberia westward.

**11. O — rice agriculture and East Asia**
- Haplogroup: **O**
- Civilization: the entire East Asian world — China, Japan, Korea,
  Vietnam, Southeast Asia. O is the dominant haplogroup across
  this whole zone.
- Language: **Sino-Tibetan** (Chinese, Tibetan, Burmese),
  **Austronesian** (Malay, Polynesian, Taiwanese indigenous),
  **Austro-Asiatic** (Vietnamese, Khmer, Munda), **Kra-Dai**
  (Thai, Lao), **Hmong-Mien**. Multiple language families, one
  haplogroup — the linguistic diversity within O is much greater
  than within R.
- The parallel to G: G invents agriculture in the Near East, O
  invents rice agriculture in East Asia. Two independent
  agricultural revolutions, two haplogroups.
- The language families develop fuzzily alongside — they don't
  need to be rigidly sequenced, just present.

### Phase 5: The boomerang (ch ~150–155)

**12. P at Baikal — "Paikal"**
- Haplogroup: **P**
- Location: **Lake Baikal** — the deepest lake on earth, the QR
  divergence point. "P is Paikal."
- The branching chapter: from here, Q goes east to the Americas,
  R turns back west toward Europe.
- Q → Book of Leo (ch 221–252). Crosses Beringia. Not this gap.
- This is a pure divergence-point chapter — the moment before two
  lineages go in completely different directions.

**13. Manu and Yemo / R1a and R1b — the Yamnaya**
- Haplogroup: **R** (R1a = Indo-Iranian / Balto-Slavic; R1b =
  Western European / Celtic-Italic-Germanic)
- Language: **Proto-Indo-European** — the strongest haplogroup-to-
  language link. The Yamnaya expansion (~3000 BCE) carries both
  the haplogroups and the language across Eurasia.
- Myth: **Manu and Yemo** — the PIE founding pair. Manu = R1a,
  Yemo = R1b (or vice versa). Reflexes in every daughter
  tradition: Mannus/Ymir (Germanic), Manu/Yama (Indic),
  Romulus/Remus (Roman), Gayomart (Iranian).
- Civilization: the Yamnaya expansion displaces Cucuteni-Trypillia
  (`cucuteni-trypillia.md`), enters Europe, enters India.
- Placed after G's Neolithic chapters — G builds the world that R
  then reshapes.
- Already has `manu-yemo.md`.

**14. E returns to Africa — the loop closes**
- Haplogroup: **E**
- E re-enters Africa by land, significantly later than the
  original out-of-Africa departure. The circular migration
  completes.
- Language: E may be associated with the **Afroasiatic** spread
  back into Africa (overlaps with J's Semitic connection — the
  Afroasiatic family bridges both).
- This is a structural-shape chapter — the narrative satisfaction
  of the circle closing. Humanity left Africa as CT, and part of
  it came back as E.

### Modern reveals (NOT standalone chapters)

These are **story beats woven into other chapters**, not dedicated
haplogroup chapters. They sit alongside medieval or late Register C
material per `structure.md` §5. A chapter about Napoleon might
include his haplogroup as a beat; a chapter touching modern genetics
might include the Perry case. They don't earn their own chapters.

- **The Perry Y-chromosome case** — A00 in an African-American
  man. The deep African lineage surfacing in modernity.
- **Japanese haplogroup D** — half of Japanese men, surprisingly
  recent subclade. Connects back to the Toba chapter.
- **Hitler's haplogroup** — Y-DNA lineage controversy.
- **Napoleon's haplogroup.**

### Notes on the proposal

**Total dedicated haplogroup chapters: ~14** (at the high end of
the 7–14 budget). The modern reveals are NOT included in this
count — they're beats within other chapters, not standalone.
Some of these 14 will likely merge at the writing desk:
- I (old Europe) might fold into the Cucuteni-Trypillia chapter
  rather than being standalone.
- N (Uralic) might share a chapter with another haplogroup.
- H/L/T are already merged into one Indus chapter.
- The modern reveals might be 1–2 chapters rather than 4.

**Fuzziness is the point.** These are overlapping deep-time
processes. The sequencing follows the tree branching as a rough
spine, but the actual chapters will bleed into each other.
Language families develop alongside haplogroups without rigid
1:1 mapping. Civilizations anchor the chapters but don't
constrain them.

**Mitochondrial haplogroups** weave into the same chapters as
their Y-chromosome counterparts rather than getting a parallel
sequence. See the mitochondrial section below.

**The three chapter types** all appear in this sequence:
1. Standalone haplogroup chapters (D/Toba, C/unsplit, E/loop)
2. Haplogroup-as-civilization-layer (G/agriculture, O/rice,
   J/Semitic, R/Yamnaya)
3. Branching/splitting chapters (DE split, CF split, P/Baikal)

## Mitochondrial (female) haplogroups

The "Haplogroup X" named entries in the wikibase are the
**mitochondrial / female haplogroups** — they should be included
alongside the Y-chromosome ones. The main challenge is that
mitochondrial haplogroups are **lower resolution** than Y-chromosome
haplogroups. Not because there are more of them, but because the
data is less granular — mtDNA mutates more slowly, so the branches
are less sharply defined.

This means the female haplogroup material may be woven into the
same chapters as the Y-chromosome material rather than getting
separate chapters. The resolution difference makes it harder to
tell as sharp a story with mtDNA alone.

## What's in the wikibase data

The haplogroup tree from Adam downward is fully built in the
wikibase, with two parallel versions:
- Original coded entries (A0-T as Q1137, etc.)
- Named "Haplogroup X" entries (Q16001278 etc.) with full subclade
  detail down to specific mutations

The tree interleaves haplogroup nodes with biblical figures at
each level (e.g., Adam's children include both A0-T and Cain/Seth/
Abel). This is the structural basis for Register A and Register B
running in parallel.
