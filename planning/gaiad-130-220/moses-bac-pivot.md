# Moses and the Bronze Age Collapse — the Register B → C pivot

**Seam 2** of the gap (see `structure.md` §4). The Bronze Age Collapse
(~1177 BCE) is where the book's writing register itself changes.

## The symbolic move

**Moses synthesizes hieroglyphs into letters**, forming the alphabets
underlying modern scripts. Form mirrors content — the narrative's own
writing mode shifts within this chapter. Before BAC, the book has no
modern historiographical register available as a fallback; after BAC,
it does.

This is **the hardest part of the book to write** (Emma, explicit).
Pre-BAC material leans on mosaic / interpolative Register B. Post-BAC
can settle into proper Register C historiography.

## What the chapter integrates

- Egyptian mythology — the substrate Moses is synthesizing *from*.
- Ghost pharaohs — the half-remembered figures that bridge dynastic
  mythology and history.
- Genealogy back to the first pharaoh — the chapter carries a long
  genealogical spine, not just the alphabet beat.
- Egypt + Sinai + Canaan as a single converging zone.

## Placement

- **Candidate location:** around the midpoint of the gap (~ch 175).
  Exact chapter number is one of the three unresolved structural cuts
  in `structure.md` §8 — whether the pivot lands at the midpoint or
  earlier/later affects how much breathing room each half gets.
- The pivot is not a single-chapter hinge in isolation; Register A
  (haplogroups) keeps running alongside both halves.

## Connects to

- The broader "invention of writing" seam at ~3200 BCE (Seam 1 in
  `structure.md` §4) — Moses is the *second* writing-revolution beat,
  not the first.
- The Judaism arc (`../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §2) — Exodus as one
  of the three pivots within humanity.
- The "no global flood" framing (see `genesis-opening.md`) — by the
  time we reach Moses, the Genesis material has already been recast as
  migration, so the Exodus beat lands in a pre-prepared frame.
