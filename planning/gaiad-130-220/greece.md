# Greece — thinner mythic-cycle bridge

**Tier 4** in the mythic-cycle tiering (see `structure.md` §6) — valuable
but less clean than Aztec / Scandinavia / Japan / Rome / China.

## The documentation structure

- **Homer** and **Hesiod** at the mythic end.
- Historical polities and the classical historians (Herodotus,
  Thucydides, Xenophon) at the historical end.
- **No single work bridges mythic to historical** as cleanly as Livy
  does for Rome.

## Why the bridge is thinner

- The gap between the Homeric / Theogonic register and the
  historiographical register is **wider** and is crossed in several
  places by different kinds of authors, not by one bridging work.
- Threads like the **Spartan kings traced from Heracles** exist but are
  genealogically thin and not reinforced by a Livy-style systematic
  history of the pre-polis period.
- Mycenaean → Dark Age → Archaic transition is a real gap in the
  documentation, not just in modern knowledge. The Greeks themselves
  had partially lost the bridge.

## What the Gaiad still gets from Greece

A lot, because even a thinner bridge is load-bearing:
- Homer and the Trojan cycle feed directly into `rome.md` (Aeneas as
  the tie).
- The Jezebel → Phoenician-princess → Baal-worship → Greek mythology
  thread connects Greece to the Judaism / monotheism-tragedy arc (cf.
  `../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §2).
- Dionysus as proto-Christ, Heracles = Samson, Prometheus = Lucifer,
  Deucalion = Noah — Greece is the main site for the cross-tradition
  resonance work that undergirds the "monotheism foreclosed the
  substrate" argument.

## Gaiad handling

Greece does not get the "clean bridge" chapter Aztec or Scandinavia
gets. Instead it shows up in **multiple places** — the Trojan cycle via
Rome, the pre-monotheist substrate via Judaism, the mythic-cosmology
register throughout. The thinness is managed by distribution.
