# The Second Temple — its own thing

**Structural decision.** The Second Temple period (539 BCE – 70 CE)
is worthy of its own chapter, not absorbed into the prophetic movement
or the Bible compilation chapter.

## Why it stands alone

- The Second Temple period is where Judaism **becomes Judaism** as a
  recognizable, portable, text-centered religion. The exile forced the
  prophetic program into practice; the Second Temple period is where
  that practice becomes institutional.
- The Temple itself matters — its construction (Zerubbabel, then
  Herod's expansion), its function as the center of Jewish life, and
  its destruction (70 CE) are all structurally significant.
- The period contains material that doesn't fit cleanly into the
  prophetic-movement chapter or the Bible chapter: the Maccabean
  revolt, Hellenistic Judaism, the Pharisees/Sadducees/Essenes split,
  the Dead Sea Scrolls community, the context that produces Jesus.

## What the chapter covers

- **The return from exile** — Cyrus's decree, Zerubbabel's temple.
  The Persian context (Cyrus as liberator) connects to `persia.md`.
- **Hellenistic Judaism** — Alexander's conquest, the Seleucids,
  the Septuagint as a translation event.
- **The Maccabean revolt** — Antiochus IV, the desecration,
  Hanukkah. A political-military episode that matters for Jewish
  self-understanding.
- **Herod's Temple** — the monumental expansion. The Temple that
  Jesus knew.
- **The destruction (70 CE)** — the Roman siege, Titus, the end of
  the sacrificial system. This is one of the hinge events in the
  entire arc — after this, Judaism is permanently diasporic and
  text-centered rather than temple-centered.
- **The sectarian landscape** — Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes,
  Zealots. The context that produces both Jesus and Rabbinic Judaism.

## What it sets up

- **`jesus.md`** — Jesus emerges from this world. The Second Temple
  chapter is the immediate context.
- **`bustanai.md`** — the post-destruction diaspora eventually
  produces the Exilarchate, which leads to Bustanai.
- The Rabbinic period (Mishnah, Talmud) — whether this gets its own
  chapter or is absorbed elsewhere is open.

## Relation to neighbors

- **`prophetic-movement.md`** — the prophets' program produces the
  conditions; the Second Temple chapter lives in the result.
- **`persia.md`** — Cyrus's decree is the beginning.
- **`rome.md`** — Rome destroys it.
- **`bible.md`** — the canonization and compilation of the Hebrew
  Bible overlaps with the Second Temple period.
- **`jesus.md`** — Jesus is a Second Temple Jew.
