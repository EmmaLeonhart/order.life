# Bronze Age Collapse — Trojan War and Exodus as one event

**Structural decision (firm).** The Bronze Age Collapse (~1177 BCE) is
treated as a single interconnected catastrophe in which the Trojan War
and the Exodus are **directly connected**, not parallel events that
happen to coincide.

## The connection

The BAC is already the register-shift pivot (see `moses-bac-pivot.md`).
This note adds the structural claim that the Trojan War and the Exodus
are not merely contemporaneous — they are causally linked through the
collapse.

- The **Trojan War** is one theater of the late Bronze Age systems
  failure — Sea Peoples, trade network collapse, palatial destruction.
- The **Exodus** happens in or near the same disturbance. The Jewish
  people were being particularly maltreated **because of** the beginnings
  of the Bronze Age Collapse — imperial stress, resource scarcity,
  internal pressures on Egypt's labor systems.
- The Gaiad treats this not as coincidence but as a single event viewed
  from two perspectives: Greek/Anatolian (Troy) and Egyptian/Levantine
  (Exodus). The Mediterranean world is breaking apart, and both
  narratives are fragments of the same fracture.

## Why this matters for the narrative

The deflationary-polytheism frame (`gaiad_religious_framework.md`)
requires showing continuity across traditions. The BAC is the strongest
case: the foundational narratives of Greek civilization (the Trojan
cycle) and Jewish civilization (the Exodus) are **the same crisis**
experienced by different peoples on different shores of the same sea.

This is not a modern scholarly observation being imported — the
genealogical data makes it concrete. The Charlemagne ancestry
(`charlemagne_to_adam.md`) runs through both the Trojan line (Aeneas →
Alban kings → Rome) and the Israelite line. They converge in the same
generations around the BAC era.

## What the chapter does

- Names the BAC as a systemic collapse, not just a backdrop.
- Places the Trojan War (Priam, Hector, Aeneas) and the Exodus (Moses,
  Ramesses/Merneptah era) as two faces of the same event.
- The Jewish mistreatment angle: Egypt under late-Ramesside stress is
  not a timeless tyranny but a specific failing state squeezing its
  labor populations harder as the system crumbles.
- Connects forward: Troy → Aeneas → Rome (`rome.md`); Exodus → Moses →
  alphabet synthesis (`moses-bac-pivot.md`) → Torah (`torah.md`).
- Connects backward: the civilizations destroyed (Hittites, Mycenaeans)
  had genealogical chains in the data that end here. The BAC is where
  several branches of the pruned tree terminate.

## Structural vs. vibe

This is a **structural** decision — the Trojan War and Exodus are
connected in the chapter plan, not just thematically resonant. A chapter
(or chapter sequence) must physically place these narratives adjacent to
each other and name the connection. This is not background atmosphere;
it's a plot point.
