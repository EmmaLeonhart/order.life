# The Bible — dedicated chapter(s), with structural omissions

The Bible gets a **dedicated chapter** in the gap. Separate from the
Torah-specific handling (`torah.md`) and from David (`david.md`); this
file covers the Bible as a **compiled object** and the Gaiad's overall
approach to biblical material.

## What this chapter is about

- The Bible **as a compiled thing** — how it came to be the document
  Christians and Jews inherit, including canonicity decisions,
  post-exilic redaction, Masoretic vs. Septuagint traditions, the
  long tail of apocrypha.
- The compilation story is adjacent to `moses-bac-pivot.md` (Moses
  synthesizing writing at the BAC) but occurs later in book-time —
  the *editing* of the text, not its generative moment.

## Coverage philosophy

- Substantial coverage overall. The Judaism-most-material-of-any-
  religion framing in `../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §2 applies.
- David gets **at least one chapter** dedicated to him. See
  `david.md`.
- Torah gets its own treatment. See `torah.md`.
- Broader Old Testament material (prophets, exile, return) carries
  weight because the monotheism-tragedy arc runs through it.

## Deliberate omission: Judges

**The Book of Judges is largely skipped.** Not considered important
enough for the Gaiad's purposes — the period's material doesn't pay
its own way given chapter scarcity. This is a decision, not an
oversight.

Consequences:
- Direct-line chapter progression can jump from Moses / Joshua / the
  settlement material to the United Monarchy (David, Solomon) without
  a Judges stopover.
- Individual judge-figures (Samson, Deborah, Gideon) may still appear
  briefly in other chapters' texture, but the Judges-era cycle of
  apostasy-judge-deliverance is not structurally load-bearing here.
- The Samson = Heracles cross-tradition thread from
  `../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §2 survives this — it can travel
  into the Greek / Mediterranean-substrate chapters rather than
  needing its own Judges chapter.

## What a "Bible chapter" probably looks like

Still unsettled. Candidates:
- **Compilation-focused** — the canonization story, post-exilic
  editing, Council of Jamnia, Masoretic stabilization.
- **Structural-focused** — how the Bible works as a document, why
  certain sequences survive and others don't.
- **Thematic-focused** — the arc from polytheistic substrate (Ugaritic
  El, Canaanite pantheon) to strict monotheism, which is already the
  framing in tone-and-founders. Would compress a lot of material.

Likely a blend. Exact treatment emerges at the writing desk.
