# Torah — dedicated coverage within the biblical arc

The Torah gets its own treatment separate from the broader `bible.md`
compilation chapter. The five books carry enough distinct weight —
narratively, structurally, and within the Gaiad's register scheme —
that they don't dissolve cleanly into a general "Bible chapter."

## What sits here vs. elsewhere

- **`moses-bac-pivot.md`** — Moses as the alphabet synthesizer at the
  Bronze Age Collapse. The *generative* moment. Register B → C seam.
- **`bible.md`** — the Bible as a compiled object; canonization,
  redaction, Masoretic/Septuagint. The *editing* moment.
- **`torah.md` (this file)** — the Torah as a narrative-doctrinal unit
  between those two moments. Genesis-through-Deuteronomy as the core
  spine of the Hebrew tradition.

## What the Torah does for the Gaiad

- **Genesis material is already partly absorbed** into
  `genesis-opening.md` (Adam / Cain / Abel / Seth / Noah-as-migration).
  The Torah chapter does not re-do those; it picks up after the
  patriarchal narratives and carries the Exodus / wilderness /
  law-giving arc.
- **Exodus is the Moses chapter's natural companion.** Moses at the
  BAC (pivot) and Moses leading the Exodus (Torah narrative) are
  adjacent but distinct — the alphabet-synthesis move sits at the seam,
  the Exodus narrative is Register C material using the alphabet that
  just stabilized.
- **The law-giving matters structurally.** The Sinai moment is one of
  the hinges in the Gaiad's broader monotheism-tragedy arc — codified
  monotheism becomes a portable, text-anchored identity that survives
  exile. That's the thing that makes post-exilic Judaism possible, and
  through it Christianity and Islam.

## Coverage philosophy

- Substantial. Torah gets more than a mention but isn't the whole
  Bible arc — `bible.md` handles the compiled-object story, `david.md`
  handles the United Monarchy, `torah.md` handles the five books as a
  narrative-doctrinal unit.
- The Judaism-most-material-of-any-religion framing
  (`../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §2) applies here as much as
  anywhere.

## Open questions

- Does the Torah get one chapter or a short sequence (e.g., Exodus /
  Sinai / wilderness as their own beats)? Probably a short sequence,
  but the exact count emerges at the writing desk.
- Relationship to the Samson = Heracles thread (see `bible.md` on
  Judges-skip): Samson survives but travels into the Mediterranean-
  substrate chapters, not the Torah chapter. Torah ends at
  Deuteronomy; Judges-era material isn't its problem.
- Patriarchal narratives (Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Joseph) — how much
  lives here vs. gets woven into gateway-ancestor / genealogical
  material elsewhere. Abraham in particular has cross-tradition pull
  (Ishmael → Arabian lineage, see `arabia.md`) that may lift him
  partly out of a pure Torah chapter.
