# Māori — reclassified to the modern-period block

Māori / Polynesian whakapapa is a **tier-1-structural** case — unbroken
genealogy from **Io / Ranginui / Papatūānuku** through **Kupe** and the
great canoes to present-day individuals. Documentation quality is the
exception to the tier list: most of the other clean-bridge cases were
captured by indigenous-literate scribal traditions; Māori material was
captured by **19th-century colonial-era ethnography**.

This is not a weakness — it is a structural feature.

## Not in this gap

**Placement: Māori material belongs in the 253–328 block (modern
period, Virgo/Libra/Scorpius), not in 130–220.**

The transition the Māori case dramatizes is the **colonial clash
itself** — a living mythic tradition meeting European modernity. That
belongs in the modern chapters, not in the pre-modern gap.
