# China — mythic cycle via the Shiji

**Tier 3** in the mythic-cycle tiering (see `structure.md` §6). The
"smoother bridge but less vivid" case.

## The documentation structure

**Sima Qian's *Shiji*** (c. 94 BCE) is the anchor. Three Sovereigns and
Five Emperors → Xia → Shang → Zhou → historical. Continuous prose
history from mythic origins to the author's own time.

## Why it's smoother-but-less-vivid

**Confucian rationalization had already partly flattened the mythic
register** before the *Shiji* compiled it. By the time Sima Qian is
writing, the mythic material has been filtered through several
generations of scholar-officials who systematically preferred moral-
exemplar readings over wonder-tale readings. The cosmology is still
present but domesticated.

Compare:
- **Scandinavia** (`scandinavia.md`) — Christian monks recording pagan
  gods with the wildness mostly intact.
- **China** — rationalist officials recording their own ancestral myths
  *after* a philosophical tradition had already pre-digested them.

The bridge is **cleaner** (you can follow the dynastic chain without
gaps) but the mythic side is **paler**.

## Emma's working line

> "China is weird — people lived in China a lot, but China did
> modernize a lot faster and quicker."

The "modernize" here is the Confucian-rationalization move, not
industrial modernity. Chinese mythic material got transposed into a
proto-historiographical register earlier than any of the other tier-3
cases. The *Shiji* is not catching a living mythic tradition at the
threshold; it's documenting an already-philosophized one.
