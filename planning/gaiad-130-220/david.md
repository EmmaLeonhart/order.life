# David — at least one dedicated chapter

David gets **at least one chapter** in the gap. He is one of the named
people in the Old Testament arc whose treatment isn't absorbed into
`torah.md` or `bible.md` — he's his own anchor.

## Why a dedicated chapter

- **United Monarchy pivot.** David is the hinge where the biblical
  narrative moves from tribal / settlement material into state-level
  kingship. Chapter scarcity is real in this gap, but David
  structurally earns one.
- **Genealogical load-bearing.** The Davidic line carries enormous
  downstream weight — messianic claims, the Jesus genealogies in
  Matthew and Luke, the Solomonic line feeding `ethiopia.md` (Kebra
  Nagast → Menelik). Having David as a named chapter gives those later
  threads something to attach to.
- **Judaism-most-material framing** applies
  (`../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §2).

## What this chapter probably does

Still emerging, but candidate threads:
- David as a political figure — coalition-builder like Jimmu, messier
  than the tradition sometimes flattens him. Bathsheba, Uriah, the
  succession struggles.
- David as a literary/psalmic figure — the poet-king, the Psalms as
  part of the register-C corpus.
- David as genealogical node — consciously setting up downstream hooks
  (Solomon, the Kebra Nagast line, Jesus's claimed descent).

Exact blend lands at the writing desk. At least one chapter; possibly
more if the political / genealogical / literary threads won't
co-habit.

## Relation to neighbors

- **`torah.md`** — ends at Deuteronomy; David is post-Torah material,
  so this chapter picks up after the Torah sequence (with Judges
  skipped per `bible.md`).
- **`bible.md`** — David-as-edited-into-the-canon belongs there; David
  the person belongs here.
- **`jesus.md`** — claimed Davidic descent; genealogical handoff.
- **`ethiopia.md`** — Solomonic dynasty via Menelik I, son of Solomon
  and the Queen of Sheba.
