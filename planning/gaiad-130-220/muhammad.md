# Muhammad — Manichaean heir, forced into founding

Islam as a religion gets its wider treatment in
`../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §4; this file is the per-figure vision
for the gap chapter(s).

## The person

- **Not trying to start something new.** Forced into a position of
  starting something new by events.
- Much of his story is about being part of something larger than himself
  that is doing things he doesn't necessarily want — alongside ordinary
  human motives.
- The prophetic role **arrives to him** rather than being sought.
- Deflationary, but **pro-Muhammad in net**.

## Structural incorporation into the super-network

Muhammad's lineage is placed **with descent from Rome** — a deliberate
structural move that incorporates him into the West Eurasian
super-network rather than leaving Islam as an external break. He
becomes a node in the same graph as Charlemagne and Bustanai.

## Connecting threads

- **Elagabalus → Iamblichus → Emesa-stone-to-Mecca (Kaaba connection).**
  See `elagabalus.md` for the black-stone lineage from Syrian Emesa
  into the Kaaba.
- **Sol Invictus** as part of the late-Roman religious substrate that
  feeds both Christianity and, indirectly, the Islamic frame.
- **Neoplatonism** as the philosophical bridge.
- **Bustanai / Sasanian-princess Jewish-Persian bridge** — the genealogical
  half of the same structural move. See `bustanai.md`.

## Islam's arc after the founding

Islam extends Manichaeism and tries to be a universal religion. In net
it fails at that goal — behaves as a **syncretic** religion in practice.
The syncretic / universalist element erodes over time due to political
power dynamics. Founder treatment stays sympathetic; institutional
trajectory is the deflationary part.
