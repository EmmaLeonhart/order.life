# Ethiopia — the Solomonic dynasty via the Kebra Nagast

Confirmed strong case beyond the numeric tier list (see `structure.md`
§6). A continuous royal line from mythic origin to living memory — one
of the longest such chains anywhere.

## The documentation structure

- ***Kebra Nagast*** (14th c.) — compiles the Solomon / Sheba / Menelik
  tradition in Ge'ez.
- Solomonic dynasty from **Menelik I** (son of Solomon and the Queen
  of Sheba) through to **Haile Selassie** (d. 1975) — mythic origin
  through living memory.
- The chain is unusually long *and* treated as continuous by the
  tradition itself. Modern Rastafari extends it in a further direction.

## Why it's load-bearing

- The bridge is **religiously live**, not antiquarian — Ethiopian
  Orthodox Christianity carries the Solomonic claim as a live theological
  premise, not as folklore.
- Connects directly to the **Judaism arc** (see
  `../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §2) — the Solomon / Sheba material
  is biblical-adjacent and extends the Davidic line outward in a
  different direction than the Bustanai / Exilarchate thread.
- Connects to the **Noah-as-out-of-Africa** framing (see
  `genesis-opening.md`) — Ethiopia is the near-origin geography of that
  migration. The Solomonic-dynasty chain then brings a *return loop* of
  the biblical material back to the continent the story left.

## Gaiad handling

- One chapter or a dedicated section tracking the chain through time,
  with Menelik I as the mythic hinge and Haile Selassie as a point of
  arrival (not necessarily the endpoint — Rastafari lets it continue).
- Plays well adjacent to the Aksum / Red Sea trade world, which is
  also the geography of the Bab al-Mandab crossing in
  `genesis-opening.md`. The book returns to the same strait from
  opposite directions across the gap.
