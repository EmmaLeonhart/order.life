# Europe arc — unified, but tilts toward Britain

Companion to `asia-arc.md`. Europe's handling in this gap is
different in shape from Asia's:

- **Europe is more unified.** The West Eurasian super-network (see
  `structure.md`) makes Europe one continuous graph rather than four
  distinct civilizational blocks. Jesus-via-Rome, Muhammad-via-Rome,
  Charlemagne, Bustanai all connect into the same mesh. There isn't
  the same need for a single arc-shaped spine the way Asia needs one
  to hold India / China / Japan together.
- **But there's still a gentle tilt toward Britain.** The European
  material leans a bit toward Britain as its terminal node — not as a
  climax in the Asia / Japan sense, but as a *where the arc ends up*
  beat.

## Why Britain as the tilt

- **Anglosphere is downstream of everything.** Roman Britain →
  Anglo-Saxon settlement → Norman Conquest → the gradual coalescence
  of English-speaking polity. By 1453 (gap's end), England is heading
  into the position that the modern period then amplifies.
- **Gateway-ancestor math.** The Charlemagne descent line
  (`charlemagne.md`) lands heavily in the British royal and aristocratic
  population; the British Isles absorb a dense share of the
  super-network's genealogical load by the end of the gap.
- **Terminal placement.** Britain is geographically the western
  terminus of the West Eurasian super-network — the literal
  northwestern edge of the landmass the super-network covers. That
  parallels Japan as the eastern terminus of the Asia arc (see
  `japan-emphasis.md`), though the symmetry isn't exact.

## What this does NOT mean

- **Not a Britain-centric retelling.** France, Italy, the Byzantine
  East, Iberia, Scandinavia, Germany, the Slavic world all get their
  proper weight. Britain is a *tilt*, not a center of gravity.
- **No Anglo-Saxon exceptionalism.** The gentle lean toward Britain
  is structural (genealogical terminus) not ideological. The Gaiad is
  not claiming English-speaking civilization is the point Europe was
  moving toward — just noting that, as a matter of how the graph is
  drawn, a disproportionate number of lines end up intersecting in
  the British Isles by gap's end.
- **Not carried forward automatically.** Per `scope-caveats.md`,
  tilts in this gap are local to this gap. The modern period
  redistributes attention on its own terms.

## Interaction with existing files

- **`charlemagne.md`** — the Charlemagne descent line lands heavily
  in Britain; the tilt is partly a consequence of that math.
- **`jesus.md`** — Jesus-via-Rome → Theophylacts → European descent
  broadly, and then into Britain as one of the terminal destinations.
- **`marozia.md`** — X-Files peak in Italy, not Britain. The tilt is
  a tilt of terminal destination, not of dramatic concentration.
- **`scandinavia.md`** — Scandinavia stays weighted on its own terms
  via the Eddas; this isn't absorbed into a British arc.

## Guardrail

The Britain tilt is thinner than the Japan tilt. Europe is more
unified to begin with, so less work is being done by the tilt — it's
a gentle directional lean, not a restructuring. Don't over-read it.
