# Scope caveats for this gap

Meta-notes so future-me doesn't over-read tilts in this directory as
tilts of the whole epic.

## Japan bias is LOCAL to this gap

`japan-emphasis.md` and `asia-arc.md` tilt this gap toward Japan.
**That tilt does not carry forward automatically.** Later sections
(modern, contemporary) distribute attention on their own terms.
Reading backward from the gap to "the Gaiad is Japan-heavy overall"
would be a misread — the Japan emphasis is doing specific structural
work *here* (Kojiki-template, Shinto-lineage-of-Lifeism, Asia arc
climax) and does not imply the same weighting elsewhere.

## Medieval period is constraint-heavy

This gap is harder than the modern period for a specific reason:
**we have to do a lot of load-bearing religious and lineage
establishment work** here that the modern period doesn't need to do.

- The major world religions get founded / consolidated in this gap.
- The super-network genealogical spine (Charlemagne, Bustanai,
  Heo Hwang-ok, Jesus-via-Rome, Muhammad-via-Rome) has to be laid
  down here so modern chapters can rely on it.
- Gateway-ancestor ubiquity becomes structurally load-bearing via
  pedigree collapse inside this window.
- Register C has to stabilize.

This is why chapter scarcity bites harder in the gap than elsewhere —
every chapter is doing double or triple duty (narrative + religious
establishment + lineage + register work). The modern period has more
chapter room *because* the gap did the foundational work.

## Southeast Asia — handled modern, not medieval

Medieval Southeast Asia is mostly **not** covered here. Coverage
shifts to modern SE Asia (wars, Gen Z revolutions in the
contemporary period, etc.). The reasoning: medieval SE Asia doesn't
pay its own way against the establishment-work constraints above,
and modern SE Asia has more direct hooks.

Not a dismissal of medieval SE Asia's importance — a scope decision.
Good mythic material that *does* earn a chapter (see `asia-arc.md`
guardrail) can still surface, but the default is defer to modern.

## Modern material belongs in a later planning block

Topics that came up in gap conversations but belong post-1453:
- Sikhism, gunpowder empires, Mughal India, modern India arc
- Baha'i (deflationary and critical — rival-and-causes-harm framing)
- Modern Southeast Asia (Gen Z revolutions, wars, etc.)
- Islam's later diffusion into Indonesia proper as a demographic
  center of gravity

A `planning/gaiad-221-onward/` (or similar) directory will hold
these when we get there. `islam-syncretic.md` flags them at the
bottom so they're not lost.
