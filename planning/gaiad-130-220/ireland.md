# Ireland / Celtic — hard requirement

**Hard requirement.** Emma explicit: "the Celtic ones really, really
go in."

## The documentation structure

- ***Lebor Gabala Erenn*** (11th c. compilation of older material) —
  the Book of Invasions. Milesians → Tuatha De Danann → high kings of
  Tara → Brian Boru (d. 1014).
- This is a complete mythic-to-historical bridge: mythic invasions of
  Ireland → semi-legendary high kings → fully historical figures.
- The compilation is post-Christian (11th c.) recording pre-Christian
  material — structurally comparable to Snorri's Eddas for Scandinavia
  or the Kojiki for Japan.

## Why it's load-bearing

- **The Europe arc's western edge.** If Britain is the gentle tilt of
  the European arc (`europe-arc.md`), Ireland is the further-west
  extension — the last landmass before the Atlantic. Celtic material
  gives the western European thread its own mythic depth rather than
  making Britain purely an endpoint of Roman/Norman inheritance.
- **Independent mythic cycle.** The Tuatha De Danann, the Fomorians,
  the Dagda, Lugh, Cu Chulainn — this is a mythic cycle that stands
  on its own rather than being derivative of the Greco-Roman one.
- **Cross-tradition resonance.** Celtic mythology has its own set of
  echoes with Mediterranean and Near Eastern material (the sovereignty
  goddess, the otherworld journey, the cattle raid) that the
  deflationary-polytheism frame can work with.
- **Brian Boru** (d. 1014 at Clontarf) as a historical endpoint — a
  named, dateable figure who stands at the transition from mythic
  Ireland to medieval Ireland.

## Coverage scope

One chapter. The Lebor Gabala provides the spine; the Ulster Cycle
(Cu Chulainn, Deirdre) and the Fenian Cycle (Fionn mac Cumhaill) are
texture, not separate chapters. Brian Boru is the historical landing
point.

## Relation to neighbors

- **`europe-arc.md`** — Celtic material feeds the western European
  thread.
- **`scandinavia.md`** — Viking Age Ireland (Norse-Gaelic interaction,
  Dublin as a Viking foundation) creates a direct link. Brian Boru's
  Battle of Clontarf is partly a Viking-era story.
- **Wales / Arthur** (`other-mythic-cycles.md`) — the Brythonic /
  Goidelic split. Welsh material (Mabinogion, Geoffrey of Monmouth)
  is the sibling tradition.
- **`rome.md`** — Celtic Gaul, the Roman conquest of Britain; the
  Celtic world's interaction with Rome is deep.
