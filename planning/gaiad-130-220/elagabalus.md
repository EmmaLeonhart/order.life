# Elagabalus — the black stone lineage

One of the book's densest X-Files-style connective episodes: a Roman
emperor from Emesa whose black stone cult, read alongside Iamblichus
and the Kaaba, forms a hidden-but-real line from Syrian solar religion
into Islam.

## The setup

- Elagabalus (r. 218–222 CE), priest of the Emesan sun-god Elagabal,
  brings the **black stone of Emesa** to Rome as the empire's supreme
  deity for a few years.
- The attempt fails politically; he is assassinated; the stone returns
  to Emesa.
- The **Kaaba's black stone** is a later heir of the same Near-Eastern
  aniconic-black-stone cult family.

## Why the Gaiad wants it

This is the X-Files formulation in action (see
`../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §1): one weird detail is a weird detail,
but the **systematic assembly** of Elagabalus + Iamblichus +
Sol Invictus + Neoplatonism + Emesa-to-Mecca produces the tone.

Brevity amplifies the effect. The Elagabalus material is short-in-
treatment but packed densely alongside Register A haplogroup content,
which is how the subversion lands — you don't expect an Emesa-stone-
to-Kaaba connection in a passage that's partly about R1a migration.

## Connecting threads

- **Iamblichus** — Syrian Neoplatonist, theurgic tradition.
- **Sol Invictus** — late-Roman solar cult, feeds into the Christian
  Sol-Invictus / Constantine material as well.
- **Neoplatonism** — the philosophical bridge.
- **Mecca / Kaaba** — the endpoint. See `muhammad.md`.

## Placement

Sits in the Roman-imperial zone of Register C, but its narrative work
is explicitly **forward-looking toward Islam**. Probably paired with
(or adjacent to) the Muhammad chapter rather than floating alone in
a third-century-Rome chapter.
