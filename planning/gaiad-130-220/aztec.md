# Aztec — the strongest late-mythic cycle case

Probably the single strongest late-mythic bridge the Gaiad gets to
work with (see `structure.md` §6 tiering — **Tier 1**).

## Why it's the strongest

Mythic cycle captured **in the act of being destroyed** — by indigenous
informants under Spanish supervision in Sahagún's *Florentine Codex*
(1569–1585), Durán, the Boturini and Mendoza codices. This is
essentially early-modern ethnography catching a living tradition at the
threshold of its own destruction.

The documentation satisfies the "modern legible form" criterion almost
perfectly: native speakers recording native categories at native depth,
under literate supervision, at the exact moment of transition.

## Structural features that make it gold

- **Tlatoani genealogy** from Acamapichtli (~1376) through Moctezuma II
  (1520) is **fully historical** — but traces back to Huitzilopochtli
  and the migration from Aztlán.
- Short, clean mythic-to-historical arc compressed into ~150 years of
  Tenochca history that the Gaiad can carry forward as a single
  extended bridge.
- Ends at the moment of conquest, which is narratively continuous with
  the Book of Leo / indigenous-clash material (chapters 225–252). Aztec
  material sits in the gap; the conquest clash is next door in the
  already-drafted block.

## Emma's working line

The mythic cycle is being recorded *because* Cortés arrived. The
Florentine Codex exists because of the conquest, not despite it. That
conjunction is itself material — the chapter can make the
documentation-crisis visible rather than pretending the codices are
neutral ethnography.
