# Jesus — tragic founding, ultimately pro-Jesus

Christianity's wider arc lives in `../gaiad-tone-and-founders.md` §3;
this file is the per-figure vision for the Jesus chapter(s).

## Tone

- Founding depicted in a **weird, tragic light**.
- **Hesitancy about the mental health of Jesus** — strong emphasis on
  the human side.
- Despite the tragic framing, ultimately **pro-Jesus** in net.

## Structural placement: Jesus-via-Rome

Jesus is placed as a node in the West Eurasian super-network with a
**descent from Rome** framing, structurally parallel to the Muhammad-
via-Rome move (see `muhammad.md`). This incorporates the Christian
founding into the same graph as Charlemagne, Bustanai, and the Persian
royal line, rather than treating it as an external religious break.

## Comparison to the Buddha

Explicitly paired (see `buddha.md`): both figures have outcomes that
**outrun their own trajectories**. The Buddha's cult-leader
characteristics are more pronounced in the Gaiad's framing; Jesus is
less so. Both chapters lean sympathetic-but-deflationary and are
working the same structural beat from different angles.

material: papacy, early church schisms, Sol Invictus / Constantine
syncretism (see `elagabalus.md` for the adjacent solar-cult thread),
and the Pornocracy under Marozia. The Christian arc's **X-Files peak**
is the papal-corruption material (House of Theophylact ~900–960,
Marozia as kingmaker and pope-maker) — not the founding.
