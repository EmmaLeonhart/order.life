# Scandinavia — the benchmark late-mythic cycle

**Tier 2** in the mythic-cycle tiering (see `structure.md` §6). With
Japan, the canonical "clean bridge" case the Gaiad returns to as a
baseline.

## The documentation structure

Post-conversion Icelandic monks writing down the Eddas between roughly
**1000 and 1220 CE**. Active mythic cycle caught by Latin-literate
scribes at the moment of transition — the people recording it are
Christians recording the religion of their own immediate ancestors,
often of their own grandparents.

- *Poetic Edda* — older oral material redacted in the 13th c.
- *Prose Edda* (Snorri Sturluson, c. 1220) — deliberate systematization
  for skaldic apprentices, which incidentally preserves the cosmology.
- The sagas — Iceland writes itself a national literature that is
  structurally continuous with its mythic past.

## Why it's the benchmark

- **Narrow gap.** One generation from pagan to Christian scribe.
- **Literate tradition was ready.** Latin + runic + oral skaldic
  technique all converge at the same moment.
- **Native-language preservation.** Not translated into a prestige
  language of the conqueror — recorded in Old Norse by insiders.
- **Cosmology + heroes + genealogy** all preserved together, not
  fragmentary survivals.

## What the Gaiad draws from it

Odin / Thor / Loki / the Ragnarök cycle as **live material**, not as
antiquarian reconstruction. Ynglinga saga / Heimskringla as the bridge
from the Æsir into historical Scandinavian kings — structurally
parallel to how Rome does it via Aeneas (see `rome.md`) and Japan via
Amaterasu (see `jimmu.md`).
