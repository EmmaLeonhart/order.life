**Structural decision.** Working backward from Chapter 221 (The Year
of Three Falls, 1453 CE). The Mongol block's Renaissance Sunday
lands at roughly **chapter 210**, leaving chapters 211–220 (~10
chapters) for the post-Mongol / approach-to-1453 material.

## Post-Mongol / approach to 1453 (~ch 211–220)

Ten chapters covering roughly 1350–1453. This is the acceleration
zone — the modern age is already starting. Events to place:

### Confirmed / strong candidates
- **Timur** (1370–1405) — last great steppe conqueror. Connects
  back to the Mongol block (Timurid Empire as Mongol successor).
- **Lithuania Christianized** (1387) — already has
  `lithuania-counterfactual.md`. The book Lithuania never wrote.
- **Zheng He** (1405–1433) — China's brief naval reach before
  turning inward. A "what if" moment.
- **Gutenberg** (1440) — the information technology shift. Connects
  to the "modernity was already starting" thesis.
- **Ottoman rise** — the empire that takes Constantinople. Needs
  build-up for ch 221.
- **Joan of Arc** (1429) — connected to the Hundred Years' War
  ending.
- **Sengoku beginning** (~1467) — Japan's warring states. The
  Japan thread picks up again after the Kenmu Restoration.

### Possible candidates
- **Ashikaga/Muromachi Japan** — Zen flourishing, Noh theater,
  the cultural period between Kenmu and Sengoku.
- **Song dynasty fall / Ming founding** (1368) — China's response
  to the Mongol Yuan dynasty.
- **Great Schism** (1378–1417) — two/three popes. Western Church
  in crisis.
- **Hundred Years' War** highlights — Agincourt (1415), etc.
- **Early Ottoman conquests** — Balkans, Varna (1444).
- **Council of Florence** (1439) — last attempt at Christian
  reunification before Constantinople falls.

## The approach-to-1453 thesis

These chapters should feel like **acceleration**. Multiple
civilizations restructuring simultaneously. The modern age is not
triggered by Columbus (1492) — it's already underway here.
Gutenberg, the Ottoman rise, Sengoku, the post-Black-Death European
restructuring, Zheng He — these are all symptoms of a global phase
transition, not isolated events.

## What's unsettled

- Exact assignment of events to specific chapters.
- How many of the ten chapters are Renaissance-focused vs.
  dedicated to specific events (Timur, Zheng He, etc.).
- Whether any of these events share chapters or each get their own.
- The Ottoman build-up: how much does the reader need before 1453
  lands?
- Japan's Muromachi period: does it earn a chapter or is it a quiet
  gap between Kenmu and Sengoku?
