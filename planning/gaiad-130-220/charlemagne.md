# Charlemagne — the European gateway ancestor

The connective figure of the West Eurasian super-network's European
side. A huge share of modern people of European descent trace back
through Charlemagne; his chapter(s) should make that visible rather
than treating him as one king among many.

## Role in the spine

- **Gateway ancestor.** One of the historical figures through whom most
  modern people descend. Chapters must foreground the downstream fan-out,
  not just his own reign.
- **European bridge** of the genealogical spine (Carolingian / Pani line
  back through Roman antiquity). Pairs with Bustanai on the
  Jewish-Persian-Islamic side — see `bustanai.md`.
- Structural anchor for the "no omissions" rule: every modern European
  population with a traceable connection gets the descent shown.

The hypothesized centrality of Charlemagne vs. Bustanai is one of the

centrality test, or are other nodes doing more of the work?
