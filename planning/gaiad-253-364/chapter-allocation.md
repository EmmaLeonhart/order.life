# Modern Period — Block Allocation (ch 253–364)

**Total budget:** 112 chapters (ch 253–364) — the last four months of the Gaian year.
**Drafted:** 1 chapter (ch 329 — Shōwa's unofficial peace Daijōsai, WWII-end, drafted out of order as a fixed anchor).
**Unwritten:** 111 chapters.

## Ground rules

- **This file does not assign individual chapters.** Per-chapter assignment is
  how allocation turns to garbage. Each block below is a month (28 chapters),
  with a thematic spine and a list of events/threads that must appear in it.
  Event-to-chapter binding happens in the drafting pass, not here.
- **Book of Lehi (ch 221–252) is NOT part of this budget.** Lehi is the
  Americas arc (pre-Columbian through 1890s Indian Removal), closed by the
  "Unsealed Book" meta-chapter. It sits in the tail of Cancer and the whole
  of Leo. Modern Period starts fresh at Virgo (ch 253).
- **Lehi has elements in Modern but is not in Modern.** Trail of Tears (1830),
  Joseph Smith (1830), Plains Indian Wars (19th c.) are chronologically Modern
  but are narratively resolved inside Lehi. Do NOT re-cover them here. Pick up
  the Americas thread in Modern at Gilded Age / US as rising power / 1898.
- **Ch 329 is a fixed anchor.** Already drafted. The allocation works backward
  and forward from it; Scorpius lands WWII's end near the month's close.
- **Density increases toward the present.** Virgo covers ~200 years, Libra
  ~200, Scorpius ~95, Ophiuchus ~80. Each is 28 chapters.

## Virgo (ch 253–280) — Early Modern, ~1453–1650

**Spine:** The post-1453 world reorganizes around gunpowder, print, ocean trade,
and religious fracture. Four parallel gunpowder empires consolidate (Ottomans,
Safavids, Mughals, Ming/Qing). Europe turns outward and inward at once.

Events/threads that must appear in Virgo:
- **Reformation**: Luther 1517, Calvin, Anabaptists, English Reformation under
  Henry VIII / Elizabeth I, Council of Trent / Counter-Reformation, Jesuits
- **Iberian world system**: Portuguese India (Goa, Malacca), Spanish Philippines,
  Treaty of Tordesillas aftermath, Magellan's circumnavigation
- **Habsburg hegemony**: Charles V, Philip II, Dutch Revolt, Spanish Armada,
  Thirty Years' War opening (can spill into Libra)
- **Ottoman peak**: Mehmed II (already in ch 221), Selim I, Suleiman the
  Magnificent, Siege of Vienna 1529, Battle of Lepanto 1571
- **Safavid Persia**: Ismail I, Shah Abbas
- **Mughal founding**: Babur → Humayun → Akbar → Jahangir
- **Ming late / Qing rise**: Wanli decline, Manchu conquest 1644, Kangxi
- **Sengoku → Tokugawa**: Nobunaga, Hideyoshi, Ieyasu, Battle of Sekigahara
  1600, Imjin War (Korea), Christian century in Japan, Shimabara 1637
- **Scientific revolution opens**: Copernicus, Galileo, Kepler, Francis Bacon
- **Witch hunts / Inquisition** as counterweight to scientific opening
- **Shakespeare / Cervantes / Monteverdi** — vernacular flowering
- **Songhai fall 1591, African slave trade ramp-up**

Budget: 28 chapters. Dense at Reformation and Sengoku; lighter on Safavids.

## Libra (ch 281–308) — Age of Revolutions, ~1650–1850

**Spine:** The balance tips. Science becomes systematic; the state becomes
absolutist then is overthrown; the ocean becomes British; the economy becomes
industrial. Revolutions in thought, polity, and production chain into each other.

Events/threads that must appear in Libra:
- **Thirty Years' War close & Westphalia 1648** (if not in Virgo)
- **Scientific Revolution matures**: Newton, Leibniz, Royal Society, Huygens
- **Louis XIV / absolutism**: Versailles, Fronde, wars of Louis
- **English Civil War → Glorious Revolution** (1642–1688); parliamentary
  sovereignty emerges
- **Mughal peak and crack**: Shah Jahan, Aurangzeb, Marathas, Nader Shah's
  sack of Delhi 1739
- **Qing peak**: Kangxi, Yongzheng, Qianlong — "High Qing" as the apparent
  pinnacle before the 19th-c collapse
- **Tokugawa closure**: sakoku, urban culture (Edo, ukiyo-e, kabuki)
- **Peter the Great → Catherine the Great**: Russia joins Europe
- **Enlightenment**: Voltaire, Rousseau, Hume, Kant, Diderot's Encyclopédie
- **Atlantic slavery system at scale**; Haitian Revolution 1791–1804 (critical —
  first successful slave revolt state)
- **Seven Years' War 1756–63** (first world war)
- **American Revolution 1776**
- **French Revolution 1789 → Napoleonic Wars → Congress of Vienna 1815**
- **Latin American independence**: Bolívar, San Martín, ~1810–1825
- **Industrial Revolution opens in Britain**: steam, textile, coal, rail
- **Romanticism** as reaction: Goethe, Beethoven, Wordsworth
- **Opium Wars open 1839 (first)** — China's century of humiliation begins
- **Tanzimat reforms** in Ottoman Empire
- **1848 Revolutions** — the great European spring that mostly fails

Budget: 28 chapters. Heavy around French Revolution/Napoleon and the
Haitian/Latin American independence cluster. Haiti must not be skipped.

## Scorpius (ch 309–336) — The Long Crisis, ~1850–1945

**Spine:** Industrial civilization accelerates into global empire, then tears
itself apart twice. The 19th-c promise of progress collides with the 20th-c
reality of industrial war. Ch 329 lands in this month — Shōwa's peace
Daijōsai as the month's late turning-point.

Events/threads that must appear in Scorpius:
- **Taiping Rebellion 1850–64** (~20M dead, largest 19th-c war)
- **US Civil War 1861–65** (pick up the Americas thread post-Lehi)
- **Second Opium War / Meiji Restoration 1868** — Japan chooses modernization
- **Italian unification 1861 / German unification 1871**
- **Scramble for Africa** 1881–1914; Berlin Conference 1884; Congo Free State
  atrocities; Boer Wars; Ethiopia defeats Italy at Adwa 1896
- **Indian Mutiny 1857 → British Raj**
- **Sino-Japanese War 1894–95 / Russo-Japanese War 1904–05** — Japan as
  first non-European great power
- **Xinhai Revolution 1911** — end of Qing, end of Chinese imperial system
- **Belle Époque** science/arts: Darwin, Marx, Freud, Einstein, Nietzsche;
  electrification; telegraph/telephone/radio
- **WWI 1914–18**: causes, trenches, end of four empires (German, Austro-
  Hungarian, Ottoman, Russian), Armenian Genocide 1915
- **Russian Revolution 1917 → Civil War → USSR**
- **Versailles 1919** — the mis-built peace
- **Interwar**: Weimar, flu pandemic 1918, Great Depression 1929
- **Rise of fascism**: Mussolini, Hitler, Franco; Spanish Civil War 1936–39
- **Warlord era → Republic of China → Japanese invasion of China 1937**;
  Rape of Nanjing
- **Holocaust / Shoah**
- **WWII** — European theater, Pacific theater, Eastern Front
- **Hiroshima / Nagasaki 1945**
- **CH 329 (drafted) — Shōwa's peace Daijōsai, ~1946**, the symbolic
  re-consecration of the Japanese throne in peace

Budget: 28 chapters. WWI/WWII will eat ~8–10; 19th-c imperial age ~10;
revolutions/interwar ~8. Do NOT skimp on Taiping or the Scramble for Africa.

## Ophiuchus (ch 337–364) — Post-War to Present, ~1945–2026

**Spine:** The 13th month. Decolonization, Cold War, globalization, digital
age, climate/AI/present. The month that must land the book — ends at ch 364,
which is the Gaiad's final chapter.

Events/threads that must appear in Ophiuchus:
- **Immediate post-war order**: UN 1945, Bretton Woods, occupations of
  Germany/Japan, Nuremberg
- **Partition of India 1947** — ~1M dead, ~15M displaced
- **Founding of Israel 1948** — refugee waves on both sides
- **PRC founded 1949** — Mao era: Great Leap Forward (~30M dead), Cultural
  Revolution
- **Korean War 1950–53 / Vietnam War 1955–75**
- **Decolonization wave**: Ghana 1957, Algeria 1962, sub-Saharan Africa
  1960s; Bandung Conference / Non-Aligned Movement
- **Cuban Revolution 1959 → Cuban Missile Crisis 1962**
- **Civil Rights Movement** in US; MLK; apartheid in South Africa;
  anti-colonial violence (Algeria, Kenya Mau Mau, Angola/Mozambique)
- **1968** as a global moment (Prague, Paris, Mexico City, Chicago, Tokyo)
- **Space age**: Sputnik 1957, Apollo 11 1969
- **Oil crisis 1973 / stagflation / end of Bretton Woods**
- **Iranian Revolution 1979** — political Islam as global force
- **Soviet-Afghan War 1979–89**
- **Reagan/Thatcher / Gorbachev / Deng Xiaoping** — the 1980s pivot
- **Tiananmen 1989 / Fall of Berlin Wall 1989 / USSR dissolution 1991**
- **End of apartheid / Mandela 1994**
- **Rwandan Genocide 1994 / Yugoslav Wars / Srebrenica 1995**
- **Internet / WWW** emerges; personal computing becomes global
- **9/11 2001 → Afghanistan / Iraq wars**
- **China's rise as economic great power** through 2000s
- **2008 financial crisis**
- **Arab Spring 2011 / Syrian Civil War**
- **Social media era**: Facebook/Twitter/TikTok
- **Climate consciousness** hardens; IPCC reports; extreme weather
- **COVID-19 pandemic 2020–22**
- **Russian invasion of Ukraine 2022**
- **AI era** opens ~2022 onward — LLMs, generative models, alignment
  question
- **Present moment** (2026): concluding chapter(s). The Gaiad ends here,
  looking forward.

Budget: 28 chapters. Density is highest in this month — ~3 years per chapter.
The last 2–4 chapters must land the book: what is Lifeism saying, from
where we stand now? The Gaiad ends with the present opening into what
comes next.

## Cross-block reminders

- **Americas thread**: Lehi handled the continent from Beringia through 1890s.
  In Modern, the Americas re-enter at Virgo-end (colonial economies as seen
  from Europe/Africa), then properly at Scorpius (US Civil War) and onward
  (US as world power).
- **Africa**: do not concentrate Africa solely in Scorpius (Scramble). Thread
  through Virgo (slave trade scale-up, Songhai), Libra (Atlantic system,
  Haitian Revolution, Ethiopia), Scorpius (colonization and resistance —
  Ethiopia at Adwa, Zulu wars), Ophiuchus (decolonization, Rwanda,
  post-colonial developmental state, Mandela).
- **Islam**: Ottoman arc carries Virgo–Scorpius. Safavid/Mughal arcs in Virgo.
  Islamic reformism and Wahhabism in Libra. Arab nationalism and political
  Islam in Scorpius–Ophiuchus. Iran 1979 is a hinge.
- **India**: Mughal in Virgo, Mughal peak/crack in Libra, Raj + nationalism
  in Scorpius, Partition + post-independence in Ophiuchus.
- **China**: Ming→Qing in Virgo, High Qing in Libra, humiliation/Taiping/
  Xinhai in Scorpius, Mao-to-rise in Ophiuchus.
- **Japan**: Sengoku→Tokugawa in Virgo, closed Tokugawa in Libra, Meiji-to-
  WWII in Scorpius (culminating in ch 329), post-war Japan in Ophiuchus.
- **Russia**: Peter/Catherine in Libra, tsars-to-revolution in Scorpius,
  USSR and post-USSR in Ophiuchus.

## Open questions (for revision, not for this pass)

- **Is ch 329 the right anchor?** Ch 329 = Scorpius 21 ≈ Niinamesai /
  Japanese harvest-thanksgiving date in the Gregorian overlay, which is why
  Shōwa's peace Daijōsai landed here. The drafted chapter is actually a
  *global 1945 peace tableau* (V-J Day, Missouri surrender on Labor Day,
  Nuremberg, Shintō harvest, Diwali, Hanukkah returning to Warsaw, Seoul
  liberation) — richer than a narrow Daijōsai chapter. Math works: 20
  chapters for ~1850–1944, 329 as peace-pivot, 7 chapters for immediate
  post-war, then Ophiuchus opens at Cold War structure. Risk: it forces
  WWII-the-event into ch ~320–328, which is tight. Alternatives: (a) move
  the peace tableau out and make 329 a more generic harvest/thanksgiving
  chapter that doesn't depend on 1945 chronology; (b) accept the
  compression as intended — the war *should* feel compressed because peace
  is what the month wants to land on. Leaning (b), but flag as an
  architectural decision worth revisiting before drafting 309–328.
- Where exactly to place Thirty Years' War / Westphalia — late Virgo or
  early Libra? Both defensible.
- How much Americas density belongs in Scorpius once Lehi has closed?
  US Civil War is non-optional; Mexican Revolution 1910 likely needed.
  South America 19th-c less clear.
- How long a tail does the book want after ch 360? 2–3 "present-moment"
  chapters, or a single concluding chapter at 364?
- Haiti gets its own chapter (confirmed in Libra) — does it share ground
  with Toussaint, Dessalines, or stretch across the whole revolution?
- AI era placement: one chapter or a thread across the last 4–6?
