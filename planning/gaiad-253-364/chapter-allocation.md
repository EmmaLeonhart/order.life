# Modern Period — Chronological Inventory (ch 253–364)

**Total budget:** 112 chapters (ch 253–364) — the last four months of the Gaian year
(Virgo, Libra, Scorpius, Ophiuchus).
**Drafted:** 1 chapter (ch 329 — global 1945 peace tableau, anchored to the
Niinamesai / harvest-thanksgiving date in late Scorpius).
**Unwritten:** 111 chapters.

## Ground rules

- **This is enumeration, not allocation.** Do not assign items to specific
  chapters or to specific months. Figure out what belongs in the inventory
  first; slot binding happens much later.
- **Chronological list** — add events where they fit in time. If you want
  to change when a thread appears, move the entry, don't add a "this goes
  in Libra" tag.
- **Book of Lehi (ch 221–252) is NOT in this budget.** Lehi is the Americas
  arc, pre-Columbian through 1890s Indian Removal, closed by the "Unsealed
  Book" meta-chapter. Lehi *has elements* that are chronologically Modern
  (Trail of Tears 1830, Joseph Smith 1830, Plains Indian Wars) but is not
  *textually in* the Modern Period. Don't re-cover those here.
- **Ch 329 stays put** unless drafting forces otherwise. It's the global 1945
  peace tableau and it lands on the calendar-thanksgiving date; moving it
  would break both a good chapter and a structural resonance.
- **Non-obvious threads** are marked with `†`. These are items the Gaiad
  should dignify that typical history books skip or shrink. They're where
  the book earns its worldview; do not drop them first when cutting.
- **Bias:** inventory leans long. We'll prune, not pad.
- **Divisions below are suggestive, not structural.** The subheadings are
  natural breakpoints (religious wars, ancien régime, long 19th c.,
  catastrophe era, post-war) — they are NOT chapter assignments, NOT month
  assignments, NOT promises of how many entries belong in each. Do not
  divide more aggressively than logic allows, and do not treat these
  groupings as binding. They exist to make the list readable, nothing more.

## Chronological inventory

### Religious wars and first ocean systems (~1453–1650)

- Mehmed II's conquest of Constantinople, Standing on the Ugra 1480, Granada
  1492, Columbus 1492 — **already handled in ch 221–224**
- Vasco da Gama to India 1498; Portuguese trade empire in the Indian Ocean
- Safavid founding (Ismail I, 1501) — Twelver Shi'ism as state religion
- † **Guru Nanak** (1469–1539) and the founding of Sikhism — the Punjab's
  syncretic middle path; do not shrink this
- † **Wang Yangming** (1472–1529) — Ming-dynasty Neo-Confucian idealism;
  the "School of Heart-Mind" as the 16th-c East Asian philosophical pivot
- † **Morisco Spain** — converted-Muslim subculture persisting after 1492,
  crypto-Islam, eventually expelled 1609–14
- Erasmus, Thomas More, Christian humanism
- Luther's 95 Theses 1517; German Peasants' War 1525
- Charles V / Holy Roman Empire at continental scale
- † **Songhai at its peak under Askia Muhammad**; then its collapse 1591 to
  Moroccan gunpowder
- Cortés (Lehi), Pizarro (Lehi) — flagged for coordination, not re-covered
- Süleyman the Magnificent; Siege of Vienna 1529; Lepanto 1571
- Calvin in Geneva 1536; Anabaptists and Münster rebellion 1534
- Ivan IV "the Terrible" (r. 1547–84); oprichnina; beginnings of Russian
  expansion into Siberia
- † **Russian conquest of Siberia** — Yermak 1580s onward; the slow
  absorption of indigenous Siberian peoples (Khanty, Mansi, Yakut, Evenk,
  Chukchi); often compressed to a footnote
- Mughal founding: Babur 1526, Humayun, **Akbar** 1556–1605 (syncretic
  religious policy; Din-i Ilahi)
- Tokugawa formation: Nobunaga, Hideyoshi's Imjin War (Korea 1592–98),
  Ieyasu, Sekigahara 1600
- English Reformation: Henry VIII, Edward VI, Mary, Elizabeth; Spanish
  Armada 1588
- Dutch Revolt against Spain (1568–1648)
- Council of Trent 1545–63; Jesuits; Counter-Reformation
- † **Kakure Kirishitan** (Japanese hidden Christians); Shimabara Rebellion
  1637; crypto-Christianity's centuries-long survival
- Copernicus 1543; Tycho Brahe; Galileo trial 1633
- † **Witch hunts** as a thread distinct from Reformation — peak 1580–1630;
  gendered violence across confessional lines
- Shakespeare (d. 1616), Cervantes (d. 1616), Monteverdi
- Thirty Years' War 1618–48; Peace of Westphalia
- Mughal peak: Jahangir, Shah Jahan (Taj Mahal), Aurangzeb's expansion and
  overreach; Maratha rise under Shivaji (r. 1674–80)
- Qing conquest of China 1644; Kangxi's 61-year reign (1661–1722)
- Louis XIV and Versailles; the Fronde
- † **Ethiopian Jesuit crisis** — Susenyos converts to Catholicism; civil
  war; Fasilides restores Tewahedo Christianity and expels Jesuits 1632

  1688 — parliamentary sovereignty
- Scientific Revolution matures: Newton's *Principia* 1687, Leibniz, Royal
  Society, Huygens, Leeuwenhoek
- Spinoza, Descartes, Hobbes, Locke
- † **Oyo Empire** at scale; Dahomey formation; Akan states coalescing
  (Asante founded c. 1670)
- Tokugawa sakoku; Edo urban culture (ukiyo-e seeds, kabuki)
- † **Raskol / Russian Old Believers schism** (1666) — the Russian Orthodox
  fracture that the West tends to ignore
- Atlantic slave trade reaching industrial scale
- † **Quakers, Shakers, early American religious plurality**; Roger
  Williams, Anne Hutchinson
- Peter the Great (r. 1682–1725) — Russia's westward pivot

### Ancien régime and Enlightenment (~1650–1800)

*(The 17th-century entries above already pull into this arc; the ~1650
cut is approximate. Thirty Years' War, Scientific Revolution, and
Tokugawa sakoku all belong to the same long shift.)*

- † **Dzungar Genocide** by Qing (1755–58) — 70–80% of the Dzungar
  population killed; Qing's forgotten atrocity; reshapes Central Asia
- High Qing: Yongzheng, Qianlong; apparent peak before 19th-c collapse
- War of Spanish Succession; Hanoverian Britain
- † **Nader Shah's sack of Delhi 1739** — Mughal power shatters in a single
  raid; Peacock Throne seized
- Maratha Confederacy's actual governance (often skipped between Mughal and
  Raj); Third Battle of Panipat 1761
- † **Sikh Empire under Ranjit Singh** forming (early 1800s but rooted in
  the misl confederacy of the late 1700s)
- Enlightenment: Voltaire, Rousseau, Hume, Kant, Diderot, Encyclopédie
- † **Zemene Mesafint** — Ethiopia's "Age of Princes," 1769–1855; imperial
  power fragments among regional warlords; almost always skipped
- Catherine the Great; partitions of Poland 1772/1793/1795
- Seven Years' War 1756–63 — first global war
- American Revolution 1776
- † **Haitian Revolution 1791–1804** — first successful slave revolt state;
  Toussaint, Dessalines; structurally central, often undersold
- French Revolution 1789 → Terror → Directory → Napoleon
- Industrial Revolution opens in Britain; steam, textile, coal
- † **Tay Son rebellion** in Vietnam (1771–1802); Nguyen Anh's unification
- † **Wahhabism** founding — Ibn Abd al-Wahhab; alliance with Saud 1744
- † **Bábism / seeds of Bahá'í** — the Shaykhi school and anticipation of
  the Báb in Qajar Iran
- Jenner's smallpox vaccine 1796

  thread begins
