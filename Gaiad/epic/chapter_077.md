When {{c|Siberia}} had poured his basalt floods
Across two million kilometers of plain
And filled the atmosphere with carbon's suds
Of dioxide—again, and yet again—

The heat that should have radiated back
To the cold of space was captured in the thick
And thickening blanket, and the track
Of temperature rose year by year, the tick

Of degrees climbing past what life had known
Since before the Permian—and the warmth
Spread from equator up toward the zone
Of polar cold, advancing from the warmth

Of tropics outward, leaching cold from every
High-latitude refuge that had hoped
To wait it out—and {{c|Greenhouse}} very
Calmly walked the world and groped

For the last of every cooling thing:
The polar ice that once reflected sun
Back into space was melting, offering
Dark ocean-water where there had been none

That could absorb the heat instead of send
It back—and so the warming warmed
Itself beyond the need of {{c|Siberia}}'s end:
The feedback spiral was now normed

To self-sustaining—cut the volcanoes
And the warming would continue on
Through its own momentum—{{c|Greenhouse}} knows
No brake, once set in motion—going on

And on through every thermostat
That evolution had designed in cold
Or hot-adjusted physiologies, that
Kind of ancient warmth-management was old

And had no answer for what came
On this time-scale—too fast for evolution
To respond with anything the game
Of adaptation could call a solution.

Then {{c|Methane}} was released from the frozen north:
The permafrost that locked the gas below
In hydrate crystals broke, and poured it forth
In bubbling surface-ruptures, letting go

Of thirty-times-the-CO₂ of warming power
Per molecule—and so the spiral found
New fuel, and climbing through each hour
And year and century, the compound

Effect of carbon and of methane both
Made the temperature rise toward
The wet-bulb threshold—the sworn oath
Of physics that when wet-bulb temperature poured

Past thirty-five degrees, no mammal
Or warm-blooded creature in the heat
Could cool itself by sweating—the animal
Protein would denature, and defeat

Would come not from predator or drought
But from the air itself, the open shade
Of an equatorial world throughout
Which life had always breathed would now be made

Inhospitable—{{c|Heat Death}} walked the tropic zone
And made it empty of the large and warm:
The gorgonopsian on his hunting stone,
The dicynodont in its grazing form—

They could not stay in regions that had been
Their evolutionary home for all
Their generations—and they could not flee
Because the cold retreated too, and fall

Of cooling refuge northward and southward both
Had closed—the trap was set from equator
To pole: {{c|Heat Death}} had taken oath
To follow every creature like a creator

In reverse—unmake the warm-blooded
By making the world too warm to hold
The warm-blooded—{{c|Tropics}} brooded
Where empty ground replaced the green and bold.

The Permian had been a warmer world
Than what had come before—the tropics
Had been rich with synapsid life unfurled
In its best forms—and now the topics

Of conversation in the dying south
Were only: where to go, and was there
Anywhere cold enough to keep the mouth
And lungs alive in air that wouldn't tear

The protein from the cell?
There was not—not any more—
For cold had fled to where it fell
Beyond the pole and through the floor

Of sea-ice that was thinning, thinning still—
And {{c|Greenhouse}} followed every last degree
Of cold beyond the edge of every hill
And mountain where the dying tried to flee.

Honor the warning of the warming world:
That feedback loops once started do not stop
At any given point—that when unfurled
The spiral has no natural drop

Of momentum until the fuel runs out—
And the fuel of carbon in the air
Is measured in the geological-bout
Of time that takes it back: a hundred-fair-

And-thousand years of ocean-chemistry
To absorb what eruptions pour in one
Catastrophic decade—and the free
And living world must wait until it's done.
