When {{c|Siberia}} had torn his first great vents
Across the northern plain in floods of fire
And basalt-rivers bright, the consequence
Rose through the atmosphere on plumes still higher—

For sulfur dioxide poured from every crack
Of every fissure in the flooding stone
And rose into the stratosphere's own track
And there became the seed of what was sown

As {{c|Acidus}}—born within the sky
From every sulfur atom rising fast,
Who found the water in the upper high
And made of every raindrop, first to last,

A burning thing: for sulfur meeting cloud
Becomes the sulfuric acid falling slow
Toward every forest canopy and shroud
Of leaf that waited in the world below.

"I am {{c|Acidus}}," the new rain declared
As it fell burning on the ancient fern,
"And I have no intention to be spared
From touching everything—I fall and turn

Whatever I alight on toward my kind:
Acid is not selective in its work—
The leaf, the bark, the root, the seed, the rind
Of every living plant may feel my quirk."

And {{c|Sulfur}} spread his daughters through the cloud
And they fell down as acid on the green
And brown and growing world below, and loud
Was nothing—only the slow and silent scene

Of forest dying without sound or cry:
For plants do not scream—they wither where they stand
Beneath the acid, turn their leaves awry
And drop them brown upon the burning land.

The {{c|Glossopteris}} of the southern plain—
Great tongue-leaved trees of Gondwana's spread—
Felt {{c|Acidus}} falling with each rain
And turned their leaves from green to brown and dead.

For acid breaks the photosynthesis
That builds the sugars in the leaf's green cell—
Without that chemistry, no synthesis
Of food—and without food, the living fell

From vigor into weakness, weakness slow
To starvation, starvation into death—
And {{c|Withering}} walked the forest even so
And named each tree she touched as giving up its breath.

She walked with delicate and burning foot
Through forests that had canopied the sky
For fifty million years—she took the root
And made it black with leaching, made the high

And ancient trunk grow pale within its bark,
Made every leaf curl inward as it died—
And left behind her in the burning dark
A forest silhouette that had no pride

Of living in it: only the shape
Of death made vertical against the sky
Where trees had stood—no green, no rough escape
Through bark into recovery—just dry

And acid-burned anatomy remaining
Where a living forest used to breathe.
And {{c|Choking}} came to fill what was obtaining
In the air around what lay beneath

The sulfur-clouded sky—for sulfur gas
Itself, before the rain carries it down,
Burns lungs that breathe it as it comes to pass
Through atmosphere—and many a beast would drown

In air that burned them from inside their chest
Before they could flee southward from the source
Of eruption—{{c|Choking}} did the rest
That {{c|Acidus}} had not finished in its course.

Without the leaf, the photosynthesis
Falls dark—without that base, the insect starves—
Without the insect, amphibian's kiss
Of prey-catching fails—the apex carves

Through nothing—predator without a prey
Is simply large and hungry—chain by chain
The food web's bottom pulled away
And nothing kept the upper links in plane.

The ocean's phytoplankton felt it too:
For acid rain falls into ocean's face
And acidifies the surface water through
The carbonate chemistry's disgrace—

The calcium carbonate that shells
And corals use to build themselves grows thin
And porous under acid—and it tells
The reef to fall from the skeleton within.

{{c|Withering}} and {{c|Choking}} walked the land
Together with {{c|Acidus}} through the rain
And showed that the base of everything's hand—
The green and photosynthesizing chain

That feeds the whole web up to anything
That eats another—is the most fragile part
Of any ecosystem: cut the spring
Of sunlight-into-sugar at the start

And everything above it starves in turn,
From insect up to gorgonopsian down.
Honor the warning that the acid rains
First brought to what had seemed the world's full crown—
The first of Fortune's many banes
Before the worst had yet come down.
