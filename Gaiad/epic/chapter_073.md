When {{c|Phoenix}} rose from ruin's ashen floor
And walked the Changhsingian's recovered plain,
The world seemed full of promise as before—
The ocean reefs were building up again.

For after the Capitanian's cruel blow
Had stripped the middle Permian of its pride,
Five million years of slow and patient grow
Had filled the empty ocean deep and wide.

Great {{c|Diversitas}} walked with {{c|Phoenix}} there
And counted up the kinds of life with care:
The fusulinid forams built their stair
Of chambers in the limestone, free of fear.

The crinoids swayed their feathered arms in tides
Of shallow sun-lit waters, amber-bright,
And brachiopods in their calcite hides
Lay thick as pebbles in the morning light.

On land, the synapsid herds moved through the fern
In thousands—{{c|Dicynodont}} and tusk-bearer
Who stripped the {{c|Glossopteris}} at every turn
And built their lives in fullness, year by year.

The gorgonopsians on the highland plain
Hunted with the silence of the sure—
No prey was lacking in that full terrain,
No predator went hungry or impure.

And {{c|Changhsingian}} herself walked proud
Above the age that bore her ancient name—
The last of Permian's many hours, endowed
With every form that evolution came

To make through three hundred million years
Of building up from single cells to this:
The cooperative web of gears
Of eating and being eaten, not amiss

In any link from base to apex-height—
The bacterial mat that feeds the worm
That feeds the amphibian through the night
That feeds the gorgonopsian's hungry form.

Said {{c|Abundance}} as she walked the reef,
"I have never counted more kinds alive
In ocean since the Cambrian's coral grief
Was healed—the world is rich and set to thrive."

And {{c|Diversitas}} upon the land
Agreed: "The synapsid civilization
Has reached its peak—on every hand
The herds and packs are proof of our foundation."

Yet {{c|Phoenix}} felt within her ancient bones
A trembling that the others could not name—
A subtle loosening of the stones
Beneath the world's prosperity and fame.

For deep below the Siberian plain
Where none could see and none could smell or hear,
The mantle plume had gathered once again
Its pressure in the basalt's waiting sphere.

The chamber there had filled across the long
And patient centuries while life above
Rebuilt itself and grew again more strong—
Indifferent to the growing, rising love

Of pressure in the deep volcanic store
That had its own agenda, its own clock,
Its own appointment with the surface floor
Above—the plume was rising through the rock.

{{c|Phoenix}} heard it as a distant hum
Below the singing of the full world's voice—
Not loud enough to make the others dumb
With fear, but present—an intrusive noise

Beneath the harmony of living things
That told her what the others would not see:
That every peak of life eventually brings
Its own catastrophe—that the free

And fully flourishing must always know
The pressure that accumulates below.

Yet still she danced with {{c|Diversitas}} bright
And {{c|Changhsingian}} in the Permian's last light—
For what remains of beauty should be known
In full before the volcanic seeds are sown.

Honor the recovery that taught the world
To build again from ruin—for the best
Of what life was before the Dying's furled
Catastrophe was here in fullness, blessed.

Honor the peak before the fall descends,
The full world known before it disappears—
For {{c|Abundance}}'s lesson never ends:
The beautiful is worth its mortal years.
