From the ruins of the Permian civilization—
If we may call it that: the gorgonopsian
And dicynodont's sophistication
In social form, the Permian Bostonian

Of synapsid achievement at its height—
The few who'd kept the door of life ajar
Crept forward into the empty light
And found a world laid open like a scar

That had not yet begun to heal: the niches
Empty, the food-web unwritten,
The evolutionary stitches
Of a hundred million years, unbitten

And undone—a world of vacancy
That waited for whatever chance
And variation's blind democracy
Would fill it with in the recovery's dance.

Into those empty tables came
The remnant with their cellular machinery:
The molecular instruction-frame
Of evolution, the same scenery

Of genetic variation and selection
Still working in the survivors' cells—
The solution to the disconnection
Of the empty world was in the swells

Of random variation on whatever
Organisms happened still to live—
And the {{c|Archosaur}} potential, clever
And undetected, had begun to give

Its first faint signals in the diapsid line:
Great {{c|Eureps}}' descendants who had made
It through the extinction in the brine
Of the cold refugia or the shade

Of some high-altitude cold pocket—
They walked the empty world and felt
The pressure of the vacant niche, a socket
Without a plug, a table without felt

Of any occupant—and {{c|Archon}}'s line
Would read that vacancy as invitation
To the radiation that in time
Would fill the Triassic with creation.

But that was yet a chapter far ahead—
For now the world was still the mouse-
Like synapsid's domain, the small instead
Of large, the humble, the quick-house-

Building burrower who ate by night
And bred when conditions allowed
And asked of the world only the slight
Permission to continue, uncowed

By the vastness of what had been lost—
For the small do not mourn what the large
Have been before them—they have crossed
No threshold of the noble large's charge

Of memory—they simply occupy
What's there, and eat what's there, and run
From what moves faster—and thereby
They multiplied beneath the early sun

Of the Triassic's thin and acid-clearing
Sky, and carried in their jawbone still
The ancient echoes—the engineering
Of hearing already in the grill

Of extra bone behind the jaw—
The dentary growing while the quadrate
And articular had begun to draw
Back toward the ear in the embryonate

Development of the jawbone—slowly,
Over the coming fifty million years,
The mammalian ear would wholly
Emerge from what the gorgon's years

Had left as extra jaw-bone at the back—
The hearing of the mammal is the hearing
Built from synapsid bone—the track
From gorgon to the mouse is the clearing

Of the pathway of the inner ear
Through the jaw's architecture—and this
Small refinement, carrying the clear
Intent of evolution's emphasis

On sound and darkness-dwelling, would become
In time the mammal's greatest gift:
The night-adapted hearing that would drum
The world into the ear, and shift

The sensory world toward sound and smell
From sight—and in that shift would grow
The brain that processes it well,
The cortex and its radio

Of association—but all this lay
A hundred million years beyond the now
Of the empty Triassic's first cold day
Where the mouse-like synapsid bowed

To nothing, warm-blooded in the cold,
And carried what would become us forward
Through the darkness—the untold
And improbable story, coward

To nothing, survivor of everything,
The first stone of the mammalian floor.

Honor the new experiments of life
That began at the emptiest of tables—
The humble survivors free from strife
Of competition, writing their fables

Into the vacant world's open pages—
For we are the children of those small
And desperate and beautiful dark ages
That followed the Great Dying's fall.
