# Chapter 145: The Old Kingdom — Pyramids at Giza

And while {{p|Malta}} was exhausting itself on its small rocky island,
A much larger civilization on a much larger river was beginning
To build at a scale that would dwarf everything that had come before.

{{p|Egypt}}. The {{p|Nile}}. The Old Kingdom.

                                              The {{p|Nile Valley}} had
Been settled by {{c|E}}-haplogroup peoples for thousands of years.
The {{p|Predynastic}} cultures—{{p|Badari}}, {{p|Naqada}} I, II, III—had
Developed pottery, metallurgy, symbolic systems that would evolve

Into hieroglyphs, and gradually consolidated into larger political
Units along the river. By approximately thirty-one hundred BCE, the
{{p|Two Lands}}—{{p|Upper Egypt}} (the narrow river valley running from
{{p|Aswan}} to modern {{p|Cairo}}) and {{p|Lower Egypt}} (the broad {{p|Nile Delta}}

Opening onto the {{p|Mediterranean}})—had been unified under a single
Pharaoh. The founder is traditionally called {{c|Menes}}, sometimes
Identified with {{c|Narmer}}, whose ceremonial palette is one of the
Earliest documents of pharaonic kingship. The First Dynasty began.

The {{p|Early Dynastic Period}} ran from approximately thirty-one hundred
To twenty-six hundred BCE. This was the period of consolidation—
The development of the administrative apparatus, the hieroglyphic
Writing system, the religious framework of divine kingship, the

Tax-collection networks that would make pharaonic Egypt economically
Viable for the next three millennia. And then, around twenty-six
Hundred BCE, something changed. The kings of the {{p|Third Dynasty}}
And {{p|Fourth Dynasty}} began to build at an unprecedented scale.

{{c|Djoser}}, a {{p|Third Dynasty}} pharaoh, built the {{p|Step Pyramid}} at
{{p|Saqqara}}. The first monumental stone structure in {{p|Egypt}}. The
Architect, {{c|Imhotep}}—vizier, physician, priest, builder—would be
Deified in later {{p|Egyptian}} tradition as the divine craftsman. The

{{p|Step Pyramid}} was originally a mastaba—a flat rectangular tomb—
Expanded outward and upward in six stages until it became a stepped
Tower, two hundred feet high, covering fifteen acres. The first
Skyscraper. The first large-scale stone architecture. And the first

Sign of what was to come.

                                Sixty years later, {{c|Sneferu}}, founder
Of the {{p|Fourth Dynasty}}, was experimenting with true pyramid form.
{{c|Sneferu}} built three pyramids over the course of his reign—the

{{p|Meidum Pyramid}} (which partially collapsed), the {{p|Bent Pyramid}} at
{{p|Dahshur}} (whose slope had to be changed mid-construction when the
Builders realized the original angle was too steep), and the {{p|Red Pyramid}}
At {{p|Dahshur}} (the first true smooth-sided pyramid, completed

Successfully). {{c|Sneferu}}'s experiments set the stage. And then his
Son, {{c|Khufu}}, built the {{p|Great Pyramid}} at {{p|Giza}}.

                                                                The
{{p|Great Pyramid of Giza}}. Four hundred and eighty feet high. Two point
Three million limestone blocks, each weighing between two and a

Half and fifteen tons. The tallest human-made structure on earth
For thirty-eight hundred years, until the construction of {{p|Lincoln
Cathedral}} in the {{p|Middle Ages}}. The most massive structure ever
Built, by volume, in pre-industrial history. The astronomical

Alignments of the pyramid—its faces cardinally oriented to within
A fraction of a degree—suggest sophisticated surveying and
Astronomy. The internal architecture—the {{p|King's Chamber}}, the
{{p|Queen's Chamber}}, the {{p|Grand Gallery}}, the air-shafts that align

With specific stars in the sky at the time of construction—
Indicates a religious cosmology in which the dead pharaoh's
Soul would travel up through the pyramid to join the circumpolar
Stars in the northern sky. The pharaoh becomes a star. The pyramid

Is his launchpad.

                     {{c|Khufu}}'s son {{c|Khafre}} built the second pyramid
At {{p|Giza}}, slightly smaller but on slightly higher ground, so that
The two appear nearly equal in height. {{c|Khafre}} also built the

{{p|Great Sphinx}}—the massive limestone statue of a crouching lion with
A human head, carved from bedrock beside the pyramid complex. The
{{p|Sphinx}} faces east, toward the rising sun. Its human head is
Traditionally identified with {{c|Khafre}} himself. The statue is the

Largest monolithic stone sculpture in the world, and has stood
For forty-five hundred years as the guardian of the {{p|Giza}} plateau.
{{c|Menkaure}}, {{c|Khafre}}'s son, built the third and smallest of the
{{p|Giza}} pyramids. The three pyramids together—{{c|Khufu}}, {{c|Khafre}},

{{c|Menkaure}}—are one of the most recognizable architectural ensembles
In human history. They have outlasted every empire that has risen
And fallen around them. The {{p|Persian}} conquest, the {{p|Macedonian}}
Conquest, the {{p|Roman}} conquest, the {{p|Arab}} conquest, the {{p|Ottoman}}

Conquest, the {{p|British}} occupation, {{p|Egyptian}} independence—the
Pyramids have watched all of it. The pyramids are older than
History in any meaningful sense. They are the deepest monuments
Of human civilization still standing at their full scale.

And behind the pyramids is the religious imagination of the {{p|Old
Kingdom}}. The pharaoh is divine. The pharaoh is {{c|Horus}} incarnate.
When the pharaoh dies, he becomes {{c|Osiris}}, the god of the underworld
And the judge of souls. His son, the new pharaoh, becomes the new

{{c|Horus}}. The cycle of divine kingship is maintained across the
Generations. And the afterlife of the pharaoh is not merely
Personal—it is cosmic. The pharaoh's successful transition to the
Stars is what maintains the order of the universe. The pyramid is

Not just a tomb. The pyramid is the infrastructure of cosmic order.
Build it correctly, align it correctly with the stars, stock it
Correctly with grave goods and funerary texts and ritual provisions,
And the pharaoh's soul will successfully reach the heavens, and the

World will continue. Build it incorrectly, and the cosmos is at risk.
This is a high-stakes architectural project.

                                                  And the organizational
Capacity required to execute it is unprecedented. The {{p|Great Pyramid}}

Alone required a workforce of probably twenty to thirty thousand
Laborers over a twenty-year construction period. Not slaves—recent
Archaeology has shown that the workers were organized, paid, fed,
And provided with medical care; many were rotated in from villages

Across {{p|Egypt}} for temporary labor service, a kind of {{p|Egyptian}}
National-service system. The workers left graffiti on the stones
Identifying their work-crews—"{{c|Khufu}}'s Drunkards," "{{c|Khufu}}'s Friends,"
Humor and identity preserved in the limestone. The pyramids were

Built by real people, with real names, who went home afterward
To their villages. And the administrative system that coordinated
All of this—the quarries at {{p|Aswan}}, the Tura limestone quarries, the
Copper mines in the {{p|Sinai}}, the grain storage across the {{p|Nile Valley}},

The taxation system that fed the workers—was the most sophisticated
Bureaucracy the world had yet produced. The {{p|Old Kingdom}} is, among
Other things, the first large-scale bureaucratic state.

                                                             And it
Does not last forever.

                           After {{c|Menkaure}}, the royal building
Program becomes less ambitious. The pyramids of the {{p|Fifth}} and
{{p|Sixth Dynasties}} are smaller, less well-constructed, less monumentally

Ambitious. The economic basis of the pyramid-building project
Gradually erodes. Royal authority weakens. The nomarchs—the
Regional governors—become more powerful at the expense of the
Central administration. And around twenty-two hundred BCE, the

{{p|Old Kingdom}} collapses into what {{p|Egyptologists}} call the {{p|First
Intermediate Period}}. The unified state fragments. Rival dynasties
Emerge in {{p|Lower}} and {{p|Upper Egypt}}. Climate change—a drought that
Weakens the annual {{p|Nile}} flood—contributes to the decline. Famine

Strikes. The {{p|Old Kingdom}}'s grand project of divine kingship and
Cosmic-scale architecture runs aground on the rocks of climate
And political fragmentation. The pyramids remain. But the
Civilization that built them is, for several centuries, in chaos.

And the Gaiad's note here—important for the {{p|Mesopotamia}} chapter
Later—is that the {{p|First Intermediate Period}} of {{p|Egypt}} runs roughly
Parallel to the collapse of the {{p|Akkadian Empire}} of {{p|Mesopotamia}}.
The twenty-second century BCE is a crisis period for both of the

Great Bronze Age civilizations. The {{p|Akkadian Empire}} falls to
Invasions by the {{p|Gutians}}, a people from the {{p|Zagros Mountains}}.
The {{p|Gutians}} are the first great "barbarian" invaders in
{{p|Mesopotamian}} historical records. They rule {{p|Mesopotamia}} briefly

And badly, until they are expelled by a resurgent {{p|Sumerian}} dynasty.
And the climate event that weakens the {{p|Nile}} is probably the same
Climate event that weakens the {{p|Tigris-Euphrates}} flood—a severe
Multi-century drought known as the {{p|4.2-kiloyear event}}, one of the

Most significant climate shifts of the {{p|Holocene}}. The Old Kingdom
And the Akkadian Empire both fall to the same climate crisis. The
{{p|Gutians}} are one symptom. The {{p|Egyptian}} famine is another. The
Pattern of climate-induced civilization collapse is deep, and this

Is its first great instance in the historical record.

                                                          The {{p|Old
Kingdom}} ends. But what it built—the pyramids, the bureaucracy, the
Divine-kingship ideology, the religious cosmology of the afterlife—

Survives. The {{p|Middle Kingdom}}, which will rise from the ashes of
The {{p|First Intermediate Period}}, will inherit the {{p|Old Kingdom}}'s
Cultural package and carry it forward. {{p|Egyptian}} civilization is,
As a structure, one of the most durable in human history. It

Survives collapses. It absorbs invaders. It outlasts every empire
That tries to conquer it, until {{p|Christianity}} and {{p|Islam}} finally
Transform its religious content in the first millennium CE. For
Three thousand years, the {{p|Egyptian}} civilization of the {{p|Nile Valley}}

Retains its essential character.

                                     And the pyramids of {{p|Giza}} are
Still standing. The cornerstones were laid around twenty-five hundred
BCE. The faces have weathered. The limestone casing was stripped off

In the {{p|Middle Ages}} to build {{p|Cairo}}. But the core structures remain.
The {{p|Great Pyramid}} is still the largest stone building in the world
That was built before the modern era. And in the desert light at
Sunset, the pyramids glow orange and gold, exactly as they must have

Glowed at sunset four thousand six hundred years ago when
{{c|Khufu}}'s laborers laid the last capstone and stepped back to
Look at what they had built.

                                {{p|Old Kingdom}}. The pyramids.
The divine pharaoh. The first great architectural civilization

Of the historical era. The {{c|E}}-haplogroup civilization that built
In stone on a scale the rest of the world would not match for
Thousands of years. And when the climate broke, and the state
Fragmented, and the pyramids were left to weather in the sun, the

Civilization did not die—it only paused, and reassembled, and
Continued. The pyramids remain as witnesses.

{{p|Old Kingdom}}. The pyramids. {{c|Khufu}}, {{c|Khafre}}, {{c|Menkaure}}. Stand.
