In the first five million years that followed
The Great Dying's close, the world was still
A place where beauty had been swallowed
And simplicity ruled every hill

And valley—{{c|Lystrosaurus}} walked his tens
Of millions of square kilometers
Of empty land and filled the lens
Of the ecological thermometers

With his one species' mass:
Where every ecological guild
Had once been many, now the pass
Of the disaster taxon filled

The role of grazer, small prey, medium prey—
For nothing specialized enough to say
"This is my niche and mine has always been
The eating of this specific thing today"

Had survived—the specialists all died
When their specific food or prey gave way
Before them in the Great Dying's tide—
It was the generalists who'd stay.

The barrel-shaped and undiscriminating
{{c|Lystrosaurus}}, who received
Whatever plant material was rating
As available—who never grieved

The loss of any one particular
Green thing because he ate them all—
Was now the vertebrate spectacular
Of the empty world's empty hall.

And {{c|Procolophon}} ate the small
Insects that still managed to survive
In the thin and bitter world—and all
The freshwater fish that stayed alive

In cold high-altitude streams where oxygen
Still ran at pre-extinction levels
Maintained a thread of what had been
The old complexity through their revels

In the cold clear water—thin and sparse
But present, threading through the early
Triassic's universal harsh
And low-diversity austerity.

The oceans too held their simplicity—
The unity of the microbial world
Kept court in those first years, for the key
To recovery would be unfurled

Over millions of years of the bacterial
Mat's patient photosynthesis:
Slowly building oxygen material
Back into the water's synthesis,

Slowly raising the dissolved-oxygen
Content of the shallow seas until
The next tier of complexity could begin
Its rebuilding—slowly, at the drill

Of geological patience, not any year
But every millennium contributing
Its fraction of one percent more clear
And oxygenated water to the ring

Of recovery—five million years
Of this—before the fish complexity
Returned, before the arthropod re-appears
In numbers that the reef-community

Could call a reef again—five million years
Of thin and bacterial and humble
Before the flowering of the next frontiers
Of biodiversity would rumble

Back into the world.

{{c|Persistence}} walked the early Triassic plain
Beside {{c|Lystrosaurus}} and his fold
Of indiscriminate survivors—in the rain
That was no longer acid—in the cold

That was no longer runaway with heat—
For the volcanic winter followed fast
On the heels of the warming's feat:
When {{c|Siberia}}'s eruptions passed

Their peak, the ash in the stratosphere
Blocked sunlight and the world cooled fast—
From runaway warming to the fear
Of volcanic winter, the contrast

Was brutal—but the bacterial mat
Survived the cold as it had survived the heat,
For it survives through everything, and that
Persistence is the world's most basic feat.

And slowly, in the thinning of the ash,
In the slow return of filtered sunlight
Through the clearing sky, the first faint splash
Of green on bare red rock—the first slight

And tentative beginning of the plant
Community's recovery—a fern
Frond pushing through the stone, the cant
Of light upon a living thing—to turn

Toward the sun is the most ancient act
That life performs, and it was performed
Again in the Early Triassic, packed
With patience, in the cold reformed

World after the catastrophe—a fern,
A mat, a small burrowing synapsid,
A freshwater fish—these in their turn
Would be the seeds of what was rid

Of all complexity and built
From these few simple things back toward
The interlocking, beautiful, silt-
Filtering web of the restored

World that would come five million years
Hence—a new world, not the old one back:
New families, new evolutionary careers
In the new ecological track

Of the Triassic—for extinction's gift
Is the empty niche, the vacant stage,
The opportunity for the next swift
Radiation to write its age

Upon the open world—and from these few
And humble survivors of the worst
The world had made, a world entirely new
Would grow—and we are of their first

And last and only children.

Honor {{c|Lystrosaurus}} and his five-million
Year of empty-world dominion—
The first brick of the coming brilliant
And complex Triassic's foundation—
Patient, humble, indiscriminate,
Alive—the world's best denomination
When the world is desperate.
