At the Permian-Triassic boundary line
Where the rock record shows the sudden change
From complex life to simple—where the sign
Of the Great Dying runs its devastating range

Through every mountain's limestone stratigraphy—
Great {{c|Darkness}} holds dominion over all
And {{c|Void}} walks beside him through the geography
Of the post-extinction world's great fall.

For at this moment—two hundred fifty-two
Million years removed from where we stand—
The planet held less life than had been true
In any time since life had walked the land

That was not the first dawn of single cells:
Fewer kinds than at any other point
Since the Cambrian—the fossil tells
Of a world emptied past the joint

Where complex ecosystems could repair
Themselves in any ordinary time:
The coral reef, the forest—nothing there—
The vertebrate ecology's great rhyme

Of predator and prey and decomposer
All gone at once—the web not frayed
But dissolved—and {{c|Thanatos}} the closer
Of chapters stood above the landscape's grade

And said: this is the nearest that the earth
Has come to lifelessness in four
Billion years of life—the re-birth
Will take five million years or more.

Not the small and individual death
That comes to every creature in its turn—
But systemic death: the withdrawal of breath
From the categories of living, each urn

Of evolutionary achievement poured
Into the permanent—not sleeping,
Not suppressed, but gone—the record stored
In stone the only keeping

Left of what had been: the brachiopod
In limestone frozen in its last pose,
The last ammonite's coiled period,
The gorgonopsian skull—the rows

Of synapsid bones in the Permian red beds
That end at the boundary and resume
As {{c|Lystrosaurus}} only—in their treads
Through acid ground toward the triassic's tomb

Of early simplicity.

{{c|Void}} walked where every ecological guild
Had stood—the empty niches multiplied
Through every kind of living field:
The empty reef-builder's tide,

The empty mid-size grazer's plain,
The empty apex predator's domain,
The empty freshwater swimmer's lane—
A world of vacancies instead of life's great skein.

And {{c|Ending}} stood at every fossil site
Where the Permian record closes—let
The singer count what {{c|Ending}} put to night:
Ninety-six percent of marine life, and yet

There was more—seventy of the land's
Complex and vertebrate diversity
Cut off as if by geological hands
In a century of volcanic university

Of what extremity of heat and acid
Does to the living world when given time
Enough to act—and what is rancid
Cannot be unseen: the paradigm

Of the Great Dying is the test
That shows the system needs each other—
That remove the complex web and what is left
Is barely there to mother

The recovery: the bacterial mat
Is all—the microbial crust of rock
Is all that's left to hold the format
Of life against the geological clock.

Five million years the world would wear
The simplicity of disaster taxa—
Five million years of thin and bare
And low-diversity relaxa-

Tion from the complexity that made
The world worth everything it was—
Five million years to re-rebuild what's frayed
Beyond repair into what recovery does.

Honor the darkness at the nadir's floor—
Not because darkness should be desired
But because the planet came to the door
Of lifelessness and was required

To find its way back from that edge—
And did—across five million years—
And shows us that the living ledge
Is narrower than any of our fears

Can truly measure: life is less
Resilient than we tell each other—
The Great Dying's darkness is the stress
That shows how much we need one another.
