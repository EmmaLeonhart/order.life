Now let the singer pause and name the weight
Of what has passed—for there are moments when
The chronicle must stop to contemplate
The magnitude before the pen

Moves forward into the recovery's hope—
For hope that does not honor what was lost
Is shallow hope, the kind that cannot cope
With grief's full measure or the true full cost

Of what the world endured and of what kind
Of dying the Great Dying truly was:
Not any death that any living mind
Can hold in individual terms because

It was not individual—it was the death
Of categories, of the possible,
Of evolutionary lines whose breath
Had run for a hundred million years' impossible

Accumulation of refinement—
The gorgonopsian's saber and the keen
Intelligence behind it, the alignment
Of synapsid achievement in between

The coal-swamp and the acid-rain—all this
Not merely killed but permanently closed:
The door of the possible sealed to this
Entire lineage, forever imposed

With the permanence of extinction.

Great {{c|Thanatos}}—not the small and individual
Death that every living thing has kissed
Through all its days—but the primordial
And systemic death—insisted

On this distinction:

I am not the predator's swift dispatch,
Not the storm that takes the single tree—
I am the closure of the entire batch
Of evolutionary possibility—

When I have passed, no recovery
Returns what I have taken—the specific
Form is gone into the ivory
Of permanent stone—the horrific

And beautiful specific form—the exact
Proportions of the gorgonopsian's skull,
The exact construction, the exact fact
Of synapsid civilization's full

Achievement in the Permian—all this
Is not asleep but gone, and what replaces
It in the recovery is not this
But something new—the vacant places

Fill with new experiments, not old:
The archosaur who rises from the ashes
Will not be the gorgon, however bold
And powerful—different stories, different crashes.

And {{c|Siberia}} did not intend the Great Dying—
For intention is not present in the stone
That seeks its pressure's outlet, not denying
Its own nature—the basalt alone

Does what basalt does when mantle-pressure
Builds beyond the crust's ability to hold:
It erupts—indifferent to the measure
Of whatever living things the cold

And warm world holds above it—and the cost
Is purely accidental to the rock—
The genocide of the Permian lost
Is geology's indifferent knock

Of deep-earth pressure finding its release
In eruption that the surface world above
Must bear without appeal—no priest
Or prayer or sacrifice or love

Can hold the basalt back once it begins.

And so {{c|Judgment}} is the wrong word—
But in the crease of human reckoning,
The word we reach for is the word
That gives the terrible its beckoning

Of meaning: what did ninety-six percent
Of marine life deserve in their extinction?
Nothing—they deserved no punishment, no rent
Of cosmic justice—only the distinction

Of bad geological luck—of living
On a planet with a mantle plume
Beneath a future basalt-plain, and giving
No offense to justify the doom

That fell on them from chemistry alone.

We are the children of those who survived—
The mouse-like synapsids on the stone
Of the Early Triassic, who arrived

In the empty world as the improbable
Heirs of the gorgonopsian's blood
Carried forward in the small and humble
Warm-blooded, fur-bearing, in the mud

Of the post-extinction's first cold dawn—
We carry in our cells the memory
Of the cellular endurance drawn
From five hundred million years of history

That the Great Dying tested and found true:
That life, reduced to its most cellular
And basic, is more stubborn than what blew
It past—and we are its spectacular

And late and lucky progeny.

Honor the Great Dying not with silence
But with the full weight of its misery—
For the forward path is paved with the resilience
Of those who barely made it through,
And we are their improbable children.
