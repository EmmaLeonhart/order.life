Now hear the elegy of those who stood
At the very end of all they'd known—
The last great hunters of the synapsid blood
Who'd ruled the Permian from their ancient throne.

Great {{c|Inostrancevia}} ultima walked
The northern plain in those declining days
When acid rains had fallen and had talked
Their damage through the forest's burning haze.

She was the greatest predator of all
The synapsid age—her saber-fangs
Were longer than a human's arm, and all
The muscle of her jaw in heavy hangs

Of power was built for what her prey
Required: the large {{c|Dicynodont}} in herds
That once had fed ten thousand of her way
Now barely numbered—gone like scattered birds

Before the approaching acid-winter wind
That stripped the {{c|Glossopteris}} of leaf
And left the grazers with no way to find
Enough food in the growing climate's grief.

She walked alone now—there were no more young
Behind her from the last of breeding seasons,
No pack-mates' calls echoing when she'd sung
Her territorial claim—for reasons

She could not name, the world had emptied out
Around her in a way she couldn't fight:
You cannot kill a drought or put to rout
A change in atmospheric chemistry by night

Or by the force of jaw—you cannot track
A sulfur cloud and bring it down to die
Beneath your saber-teeth—you cannot hack
At acid rain—and so she watched the sky

With something that in human years we'd call
Bewilderment, though her neurology
Was not built for that word—a stall
In all the practiced certainty

That ninety million years of gorgon-kind
Had built into her lineage: the sureness
Of the apex predator's designed
And perfect world was gone, and in the nearness

Of nothing to be done came something like
The recognition of the end.

Then {{c|Majesty}} walked quietly to her side
And spoke in the language of the stone:
"You are the finest thing that ever tried
The Permian's predatory throne—

The culmination of a hundred million
Years of evolution's slow perfection
Of the hunting form—each vermillion
Drop of your ancestral line's direction

Led to you: from {{c|Pelyon}}'s solar sail
Through {{c|Theraps}}'s thinning bones and quickened brain
To {{c|Gorgon}}'s house and saber-fang, the trail
Of synapsid achievement is your gain."

And {{c|Legacy}} walked behind her too
And counted what she carried in her blood—
A hundred million years of something new
In vertebrate design, a sacred good:

"Even in extinction's final hour
You carry what will grow in darkness long
Through a hundred million years of power—
What your genes contain will carry on

In smaller forms already hiding now
Beneath the acid-bark and in the clay
Of burrows—tiny synapsids who bow
To nothing, warm-blooded, who will say

In a hundred million years: we were
The children of the last great predator,
And in the mammal's blood there is the stir
Of the gorgon's ancient corridor."

Then {{c|Farewell}} came at last—she stood beside
{{c|Inostrancevia}} in her final hours
And honored what the gorgon had supplied
To the world through all her ancient powers:

The discipline of the apex predator
Who keeps the prey herds healthy, the old
Editor of population—the debtor
Of every ecosystem is controlled

By the predator's persistent art
Of removing weakness, testing strength—
Now there was nothing left to keep apart
The weak from strong, and at full length

The empty world required no editor
Of any kind—the herds were gone,
The gorgon's role as predator and creditor
Of life's economy moved on.

{{c|Inostrancevia}} ultima laid her head
Upon the Permian stone in dignity—
Not fleeing, not despairing of the dead
World around her—in serenity

She met the end that came for all her kind
As the great have met all endings: clear,
And present, and without the blind
Refusal of the fact that she was here

At the last moment of her lineage—
And how one dies is how one's story lives
In the fossil record of the age:
The bone in stone the only gift she gives

To those who come ten thousand years
Beyond her, find her skull, and say:
"This was the greatest—now nothing she fears,
Nor ever shall again till judgment day."

Honor the last gorgons on their throne
Of ancient bone—the finest hunters known
To the Permian world, who made the stone
Remember them in dignity, alone.
