After the Darkness came the {{c|Ember}} first—
Not fire, not forest, not the world renewed
In any way that satisfied the thirst
For what had been—just {{c|Ember}}, barely glued

To living in the Early Triassic air
That was still warm, still carbon-rich, still short
Of oxygen enough for those who'd care
To need the clear and well-oxygenated sort.

{{c|Ember}} was a simple thing: the glow
Of mitochondria in the small
And barrel-shaped {{c|Lystrosaurus}}, slow
And bovine in his ways, who ate what fall

Of vegetation managed still to grow
In the acid-tainted, ash-filtered light
Of those first years—he did not know
What beauty was or speed or fight

For territory—he was simply there
In numbers filling every open plain:
Where nothing else could live or breathe the air
{{c|Lystrosaurus}} walked in his uncouth domain.

Not beautiful, not fast, not fierce—but there
In numbers that the empty world made beautiful
By default: the disaster taxon's prayer
Is not for excellence but for the full

Occupation of the vacant space
Before the complex things arrive—
To be the first weed in the empty place,
To prove that something here is still alive.

{{c|Persistence}} walked beside him through the years—
Not the Persistence of the hero
Facing the impossible—it clears
The mind to see that Persistence here is zero

Point nothing: not the will of the dramatic,
Not courage, not the noble overcoming—
But the Persistence of the automatic
Cellular machinery drumming

Its ancient rhythm in the heat:
Divide, and eat, and move to warmer ground,
And eat again—the cellular heartbeat
Of a world that lost most things but found

That the most basic things do not require
The complex world to function: digestion runs
In acid world, the metabolic fire
Burns in thin-oxygen, the breeding sons

And daughters multiply when nothing blocks
The breeding—and with nothing to compete
Against, the simplest forms unlock
A world of reproduction where they eat

And multiply unchecked across the plains
Of the Early Triassic's empty land—
The disaster taxon's dividend remains:
The first to occupy the vacant strand.

{{c|Dawn}} appeared first at the fifth million year
Of the recovery—not the sunrise
Of the pre-extinction world, but clear
And first-light tentative—the size

Of a small fish in shallow water
Where oxygen had climbed back toward
The old levels—the biological order
Was being slowly, painfully restored

By the patient chemistry of the mat:
Through five million years of bacterial
Respiration, slow and flat
And invisible, the material

Of photosynthesis had been pumping
Oxygen back into the depleted sea—
And with more oxygen the jumping
Of metabolic rates set free

The possibility of larger cells
And more complex organisms—and with those
Came creatures past the mat—and history tells
Its patient story once again: life grows

From the simple back toward the complex,
From the single cell back toward the web
Of eating and being eaten, the complex
Architecture of the food chain's ebb

And flow—but slowly, slowly, slowly
Through five million years of thin beginning
Before the world could claim the holy
Recovery of complex living.

{{c|Ember}} teaches us that the smallest room
Of possibility—the barely-free
And barely-living—holds the future's womb
If the future waits long enough to be.

Honor {{c|Lystrosaurus}} in his ugly grace,
The disaster taxon who held the door
For everything that followed—taking his place
As the humble first stone of the floor

On which complexity would build again:
Not by excellence but by being first,
Not by victory but by the plain
And patient occupation of the worst

And emptiest of worlds—the one who holds
The door open after catastrophe
Is often not the finest—but the cold
And patient and persistent, simply free

To be alive when nothing else remains.
