# Chapter 143: E — Returns to Africa

And the loop closed.

                          {{c|Haplogroup E}} is the haplogroup that
Left Africa, crossed the {{p|Bab al-Mandab}} with {{c|Noah}}, wandered
{{p|Eurasia}} for some thousands of years, and then—remarkably, almost
Uniquely among the out-of-Africa haplogroups—turned around and

Went back. {{c|E}} is the loop-closer. {{c|E}} is the one that walked
Out of Africa and then walked back in. Every other haplogroup
That left Africa stayed out. {{c|D}} went to Japan and Tibet. {{c|C}}
Went to Australia and the steppe. {{c|F}} went to the {{p|Near East}}

And branched into every other haplogroup. They all went east,
North, west—they never came back. Except {{c|E}}.

                                                      {{c|E}} is the
Haplogroup that, having wandered north from {{p|Yemen}} into the
{{p|Levant}}, turned south again. Down through the {{p|Sinai Peninsula}}.

Across the {{p|Isthmus of Suez}}. Into {{p|Egypt}}. Into the {{p|Nile Valley}}.
And from {{p|Egypt}}, across the {{p|Sahara}} (which, at the time of the
{{c|E}} migration, was a wetter and more hospitable place than it
Is today—the "{{p|Green Sahara}}" of the {{p|African Humid Period}}, with

Lakes and rivers and grasslands where there is now sand). {{c|E}} spread
Across {{p|North Africa}}, down into {{p|West Africa}}, across into
{{p|East Africa}}. {{c|E}} became, eventually, the dominant haplogroup
Of most of sub-Saharan {{p|Africa}}.

                                       And this is where the Gaiad's
Story of circular migration finds its completion. Humanity left
Africa, some of it. Wandered. Branched. Populated every continent.
And one branch of the {{c|CT}} lineage—the {{c|E}} branch—came back. The

Circle closed.

                 And when {{c|E}} came back to Africa, {{c|E}} did
Not find an empty continent. The haplogroups that had never left—
{{c|A}} and {{c|B}}—were still there. {{c|A}} was in the deep African
Interior, carried by the {{p|San}} and {{p|Khoisan}} peoples of the

{{p|Kalahari}}, by the {{p|Mbuti}} of the {{p|Congo}} rainforest, by the most
Ancient lineages of the continent's human population. {{c|B}} was
Also in the deep interior, carried by other forest-dwelling and
Arid-land peoples. And now {{c|E}}—returning from the {{p|Eurasian}}

Branch of the tree, having branched off the {{c|DE}} stem in the
{{p|Levant}} somewhere around sixty thousand years ago, having
Wandered for tens of thousands of years—returned and began to
Spread. And {{c|E}} was more numerous, more aggressive, more tied

To agriculture and to later population expansions, than {{c|A}} or {{c|B}}.
{{c|E}} pushed {{c|A}} and {{c|B}} into smaller and smaller territories.
The {{p|San}} today live in the {{p|Kalahari}} Desert because that is where
{{c|E}} did not reach. The {{p|Pygmies}} today live in the deep forest

Because that is where {{c|E}} did not reach. {{c|E}} took the grasslands,
The river valleys, the rich agricultural land. {{c|A}} and {{c|B}} took
The remnant wild zones. The pattern is familiar: the later, more
Numerous, more aggressive lineage displaces the earlier one. And

The displacement is the story of most of {{p|Africa}}'s pre-colonial
Population history.

                       But the displacement is also the restoration.
Because {{c|E}}, in genetic terms, is {{p|African}}. {{c|E}}'s ancestor—the
{{c|DE}} node, the split from the {{c|CF}} branch—was out of Africa, yes,

But {{c|E}} itself, as a distinct haplogroup, developed its identity
In {{p|Africa}} or on its border. The return to Africa is the return
To the continent where the human story began. The loop-closer
Is the haplogroup that says: we are African.

                                                    Ninety percent or
More of all sub-Saharan {{p|African}} men today carry {{c|E}}. {{c|E}} is the
Overwhelming majority haplogroup of modern {{p|Africa}}. The {{p|Bantu}}
Peoples—who themselves underwent a great expansion starting around

Three thousand years ago from their homeland in what is now
{{p|Cameroon}}/{{p|Nigeria}}, spreading {{p|Bantu}}-speaking populations across
Central, Eastern, and Southern {{p|Africa}}—are {{c|E}}. Specifically, a
Sub-clade called {{c|E-V38}} or {{c|E1b1a}} is the {{p|Bantu}} expansion

Signature. The {{p|Bantu}} migration reshaped sub-Saharan {{p|Africa}} the
Way the {{p|Yamnaya}} expansion reshaped {{p|Europe}}—a demographically
Massive, linguistically unifying event that made most of the
Continent speak related languages. The {{p|Bantu}} languages, from

{{p|Swahili}} to {{p|Zulu}} to {{p|Kikuyu}} to {{p|Kinyarwanda}}, are all descended
From the same Proto-Bantu spoken by a relatively small population
In what is now the {{p|Cameroon}}/{{p|Nigeria}} border region around three
Thousand years ago. And the {{c|E-V38}} haplogroup is the genetic

Signature of that expansion.

                                 In the north, a different sub-clade
Of {{c|E}} dominates. {{c|E-M78}} and {{c|E-M81}} are the {{p|North African}}
And {{p|Horn of Africa}} signatures. The {{p|Berbers}} of {{p|Morocco}}, {{p|Algeria}},

{{p|Tunisia}}, and {{p|Libya}} carry very high frequencies of {{c|E-M81}}. The
{{p|Egyptians}}, both ancient and modern, carry {{c|E-M78}} and related
Sub-clades. The {{p|Ethiopians}} and {{p|Eritreans}} and {{p|Somalis}} carry their
Own {{c|E}} sub-clades. And these are the populations that would, in

Historical times, participate in the {{p|Afroasiatic}} language family—
Which includes {{p|Semitic}} (overlapping with {{c|J}}), {{p|Egyptian}}, {{p|Berber}},
{{p|Cushitic}}, {{p|Chadic}}, and {{p|Omotic}}. {{c|E}} is the main {{p|African}}-side
Carrier of {{p|Afroasiatic}}, while {{c|J}} is the main {{p|Asian}}-side carrier.

The two haplogroups between them span the entire {{p|Afroasiatic}} zone,
And the language family's unity across {{p|Africa}} and the {{p|Near East}}
Is held together by the historical contact between these two
Populations. The {{p|Egyptian}} language, specifically, is {{c|E}}-carried.

The {{p|Old Kingdom}} pharaohs were {{c|E}}. The builders of the pyramids
Were {{c|E}}. Every dynasty of ancient {{p|Egypt}}, from the {{p|First Dynasty}}
Through the {{p|Ptolemaic}} period, was populated by men whose {{p|Y}}-chromosomes
Carried some sub-clade of {{c|E}}. And the {{p|Egyptian}} civilization

Is, in the Gaiad's reading, fundamentally an {{c|E}}-civilization. The
First great African civilization of the historical record. The
Civilization that the biblical {{p|Israelites}} will be enslaved in.
The civilization that {{c|Akhenaten}} will briefly monotheize and then

Fall back from. The civilization whose religious imagery—animal-
Headed gods, solar disks, underworld journeys of the soul—will
Influence the religious imagination of every subsequent {{p|Mediterranean}}
Culture. {{p|Egypt}}'s {{c|E}} signature is the proof that the loop closed.

The loop closes here. The CT lineage left {{p|Africa}} some sixty or
Seventy thousand years ago. A portion of the lineage, under the
Name {{c|E}}, came back. And the {{c|E}} descendants built {{p|Egypt}}, built
The {{p|Bantu}} expansion, built most of what would become sub-Saharan

{{p|African}} civilization, built the {{p|Horn of Africa}} kingdoms of {{p|Aksum}}
And {{p|Ethiopia}} (the {{p|Solomonic dynasty}} is {{c|E}}-carried), built the
{{p|Berber}} kingdoms of {{p|North Africa}} that would later become part
Of the {{p|Islamic}} caliphate. {{c|E}} is the {{p|African}} haplogroup in the

Historical sense—not {{c|A}} or {{c|B}}, which are older but more
Marginalized, but {{c|E}}, which built the continent's civilizations.

And the circle closes on the genealogical tree.

                                                    {{c|E}} re-entered
{{p|Africa}}. {{c|E}} made {{p|Egypt}}. {{c|E}} made the {{p|Bantu}} expansion.
{{c|E}} became the dominant lineage of a continent whose earliest

Haplogroups—{{c|A}} and {{c|B}}—remained in the margins. And somewhere,
At the back of the Gaiad's mind, is the awareness that {{c|E}} is also
Going to appear, in the modern world, in the {{p|African-American}}
Population of the {{p|United States}}. The descendants of the enslaved

{{p|Africans}} who were brought across the {{p|Atlantic}} in the transatlantic
Slave trade are, in substantial majority, carriers of {{c|E}}-sub-clades.
{{c|Malcolm X}}, {{c|Martin Luther King Jr.}}, {{c|Frederick Douglass}}—if we
Could sequence their {{p|Y}}-chromosomes, we would almost certainly

Find {{c|E}}. The modern {{p|African-American}} diaspora is the {{c|E}}
Haplogroup reaching into the Western Hemisphere not via the
{{c|Q}}-carried original migration across {{p|Beringia}}, but via the
Forced middle-passage migration that began in the fifteenth century.

The loop of {{c|E}} closed when {{c|E}} returned to {{p|Africa}}. And then,
Millennia later, a cruel second loop would reopen it: {{c|E}} men taken
From {{p|Africa}} and sold into slavery in the {{p|Americas}}, where their
Descendants would build, under conditions of unimaginable cruelty,

The labor base on which much of the modern Western economy was
Constructed. That is a later chapter. Much later. But the {{c|E}}
Haplogroup is the connective tissue between the deep past of {{p|Africa}}
And the modern history of the {{p|African}} diaspora.

For now, {{c|E}} is the loop-closer. {{c|E}} is the return to the
Continent of origin. {{c|E}} is the haplogroup whose signature is
Present in every {{p|African}} civilization of the historical record.
{{c|E}} is {{p|Egypt}}. {{c|E}} is the {{p|Bantu}}. {{c|E}} is the {{p|Berber}}.

{{c|E}} is the {{p|Ethiopian}} emperor. {{c|E}} is the shamba-farmer and the
{{p|Swahili}} trader and the {{p|Hausa}} merchant and the {{p|Zulu}} warrior.

Of human migration was not one-way. The door swung both ways.

The haplogroup went out, and the haplogroup came back. The
Continent that sent humanity out into the world also received
A portion of humanity home. The loop closed. The circle was
Complete. And the human species, as a Y-chromosomal story, came

To be distributed across every continent and every climate, with
Every major haplogroup represented in multiple places, and with
{{c|E}} closing the loop by carrying a branch of the out-of-Africa
Lineage back to the continent it had left.
