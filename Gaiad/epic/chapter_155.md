# Chapter 155: The Aryan Migration

And while the {{p|Hyksos}} were settling in the {{p|Nile Delta}}, another
Foreign people was settling—halfway across the world—in another
Great river valley. The parallel is structural. Foreign populations
Entering pre-existing civilizations on two continents at the same

Time. In the west, the {{p|Hyksos}} entering {{p|Egypt}}. In the east, the
{{p|Aryans}} entering {{p|India}}. Both arriving in the eighteenth, seventeenth,
And sixteenth centuries BCE. Both bringing their own languages,
Religions, and haplogroups into territories previously dominated

By other civilizations. Both transforming the cultures they entered,
But also being transformed by those cultures. The {{p|Hyksos}} became
Partly {{p|Egyptianized}}. The {{p|Aryans}} became partly {{p|Indianized}}. Both
Inserted themselves into existing civilizational matrices and came

Out, on the other side, as something neither purely themselves nor
Purely the people they had joined.

                                           The {{p|Aryan Migration}}.

                                                                          Here
The Gaiad must be careful. The word "{{p|Aryan}}" has been abused in

Modern history—used by nineteenth and twentieth century ideologies
To construct racial hierarchies, to justify conquest and genocide,
To fabricate a pure-blood master-race that never existed. The
Gaiad rejects all of that. The "{{p|Aryan}}" people of the ancient world

Were not a "race"; they were a linguistic and cultural group—
Speakers of the {{p|Indo-Iranian}} branch of {{p|Indo-European}}, probably
Mixed in ancestry, probably a distinct elite lineage within a broader
Population, but not the "master race" of {{p|Nazi}} mythology. They were

A migrating population with a language and a religious tradition,
Nothing more supernatural than that. The word "{{p|Aryan}}" in its
Original context meant, simply, "noble" in the {{p|Indo-Iranian}}
Languages—a self-designation of the elite warrior class, not a

Racial category. The Gaiad uses the word in its original linguistic
And cultural sense, with no racial implications.

                                                        So: around
Fifteen-hundred BCE, give or take a century or two, a migration of
{{c|R1a}}-haplogroup {{p|Indo-Iranian}}-speaking peoples began moving

South from their homeland in the {{p|Central Asian}} steppes (the
{{p|Bactria-Margiana Archaeological Complex}}, or {{p|BMAC}}, was probably
A key staging ground). Some of them went southwest into what is
Now {{p|Iran}}, where they became the {{p|Iranian}} peoples—the {{p|Medes}},

The {{p|Persians}}, and the ancestors of the peoples who would produce
{{p|Zoroastrianism}}. Some of them went southeast, across the {{p|Hindu
Kush}} mountains and through the {{p|Khyber Pass}}, down into what is
Now {{p|Pakistan}} and northwestern {{p|India}}. This southeastern branch

Became the {{p|Indo-Aryan}} peoples of {{p|India}}. And they arrived in a
{{p|Punjab}} and upper {{p|Ganges}} region that was in the process of
Demographic transition—the {{p|Indus Valley}} civilization, the great
Pre-Aryan urban civilization of the subcontinent, was in the late

Stages of its collapse. The cities of {{p|Harappa}} and {{p|Mohenjo-daro}}
Had already been abandoned or were being abandoned. The population
Was dispersing eastward and southward. The {{p|Indus Valley}} was no
Longer the organized urban civilization it had been a thousand

Years earlier.

                    The {{p|Aryan}} arrival is not, in the Gaiad's
Reading, the cause of the {{p|Indus Valley}} collapse. The collapse
Was already underway before they arrived—probably driven by climate

Change (the gradual drying of the {{p|Sarasvati}} river system, the
Shifting of monsoons) and by internal political and economic
Pressures. The {{p|Aryans}} arrived into an ongoing collapse, not a
Stable civilization. They filled a partial vacuum. And—crucially—

The Gaiad reads the relationship between the incoming {{p|Aryans}}
And the pre-existing {{p|Dravidian}} and other non-{{p|Aryan}} populations
As substantially cooperative rather than violently adversarial.

This is an important departure from some earlier narratives. The

Older "{{p|Aryan invasion theory}}," developed in nineteenth-century
{{p|European}} scholarship, posited a violent conquest—chariot-driving
{{p|Aryan}} warriors destroying the peaceful {{p|Dravidian}} cities, imposing
Their language and religion by force, pushing the pre-existing

Populations south into the {{p|Deccan}} and {{p|Tamil}} regions. More recent
Scholarship has significantly revised this picture. The collapse
Of the {{p|Indus Valley}} cities preceded the {{p|Aryan}} arrival. The
Incoming {{p|Indo-Aryans}} were not a massive invasion force but a

Gradual trickle of migrating populations over several centuries.
Genetic studies show substantial admixture, not replacement—the
{{p|Indian}} population today is a blend of pre-{{p|Aryan}} ancestry (primarily
{{c|H}}, {{c|L}}, {{c|R2a}}, and other haplogroups associated with the {{p|Indus

Valley}} populations) and {{p|Aryan}} ancestry ({{c|R1a}}-M417, with its
Characteristic steppe-pastoralist signature). The mixing happened.
It was not an extermination. It was not even really a conquest in
The traditional military sense. It was a long, complex, mutually

Transformative encounter.

                                  And the cultural results of that
Encounter produced what became classical {{p|Hindu}} civilization. The
{{p|Vedic}} hymns—the {{p|Rig Veda}} in particular, composed between

Approximately fifteen-hundred and twelve-hundred BCE in the
{{p|Punjab}} and upper {{p|Ganges}} region—are the oldest surviving
Indo-European religious texts. They preserve the {{p|Aryan}} religious
Imagination in its most archaic form: sky-father {{c|Dyaus}}, storm-god

{{c|Indra}}, fire-god {{c|Agni}}, soma-intoxication ritual, horse sacrifice,
Elaborate priestly hymns addressed to an array of celestial and
Atmospheric deities. The {{p|Vedic}} religion is recognizably {{p|Indo-
European}}—the same pantheon structure as {{p|Greek}}, {{p|Roman}}, and

{{p|Germanic}} religion, just with different names for the gods. {{c|Dyaus}}
Is the same as {{c|Zeus}} and {{c|Jupiter}} and {{c|Tyr}} ({{p|German}})—all
Descended from Proto-Indo-European {{p|Dyeus Phter}} ("sky father").
{{c|Indra}} is cognate with the Greek {{c|Heracles}} in his storm-god

Aspects. The {{p|Vedas}} are {{p|Aryan}} religion in its pure form.

But even within the {{p|Rig Veda}}, the encounter with pre-{{p|Aryan}}
Populations is visible. The hymns mention "{{c|Dasyus}}" and "{{c|Dasas}}"—
The indigenous peoples the {{p|Aryans}} encountered—sometimes as

Enemies but often as subjects, servants, and fellow participants
In {{p|Indian}} society. Some {{p|Aryan}} deities show syncretic features
That suggest influence from pre-{{p|Aryan}} religious traditions. {{c|Rudra}}
—the wild and dangerous storm-and-mountain god who would eventually

Become {{c|Shiva}}—has features that don't quite fit the standard
{{p|Indo-European}} pantheon. {{c|Rudra}}/{{c|Shiva}} is probably, in part, a
Pre-{{p|Aryan}} deity absorbed into the {{p|Vedic}} pantheon. The
{{c|Shiva lingam}}—the iconic phallic emblem of {{c|Shiva}}-worship—may

Descend from pre-{{p|Aryan}} {{p|Indus Valley}} cultic objects. (A number of
Conical and cylindrical stone objects found at {{p|Mohenjo-daro}} and
Other {{p|Indus Valley}} sites have been interpreted as possible early
{{p|lingas}}, though the interpretation is not certain.) The worship

Of {{c|Shiva}} is, in one reading, a pre-{{p|Aryan}} {{p|Indus Valley}} tradition
That survived the civilizational collapse, was absorbed into the
Incoming {{p|Aryan}} religion as {{c|Rudra}}, and gradually grew in importance
Until, by the classical period, {{c|Shiva}} was one of the three great

Gods of {{p|Hinduism}} alongside {{c|Vishnu}} and {{c|Brahma}}. If so, then
{{p|Hindu}} civilization is not a pure {{p|Aryan}} religious civilization—
It is a synthesis, with {{p|Indus Valley}} elements preserved within
{{p|Aryan}} frameworks. The continuity is real. The civilization

Did not simply end and restart; it flowed through, absorbed, and
Continued to carry pre-existing religious content under new names
And in new frameworks.

                            And the caste system ({{p|varna}}) emerges
In this encounter. The four-{{p|varna}} scheme of the {{p|Rig Veda}}—

{{p|Brahmins}} (priests), {{p|Kshatriyas}} (warriors), {{p|Vaishyas}} (merchants
And farmers), and {{p|Shudras}} (servants)—is the {{p|Indo-European}}
Tripartite-plus-one social ideology familiar from other {{p|Indo-
European}} societies, extended with a fourth group for the non-

{{p|Aryan}} subject population. The {{p|Shudras}} are originally the
Pre-{{p|Aryan}} populations, accepted into the social system at its
Lowest rank. Over subsequent centuries, this four-fold scheme
Would be elaborated into the thousands of sub-castes ({{p|jatis}})

That make up the classical {{p|Indian}} caste structure. The caste
System is, in its origins, the social codification of the {{p|Aryan}}-
Non-{{p|Aryan}} encounter—a rigid hierarchy that preserved the
Distinction between the incoming population and the pre-existing

One. The caste system's later history—its ossification, its
Religious legitimation through the doctrine of {{p|karma}} and
Rebirth, its role in keeping {{p|Indian}} civilization structurally
Stagnant for much of its later history—is covered in the "{{p|India

Stagnation}}" chapter much later. Here it is just noted as one of
The structural inheritances of the {{p|Aryan Migration}} period.

                                                                         And
Language. The {{p|Aryans}} brought {{p|Sanskrit}}—or the early version of
It that linguists call {{p|Old Indo-Aryan}} or {{p|Vedic Sanskrit}}. This

Language, over subsequent centuries, became the sacred and classical
Language of {{p|Indian}} civilization. From {{p|Sanskrit}} descended the
Modern {{p|Indo-Aryan}} languages: {{p|Hindi}}, {{p|Urdu}}, {{p|Bengali}}, {{p|Punjabi}},
{{p|Marathi}}, {{p|Gujarati}}, and many others. More than a billion people

Today speak an {{p|Indo-Aryan}} language as their native tongue. That
Linguistic heritage is the {{p|Aryan Migration}}'s most durable legacy.
The {{p|Dravidian}} languages of the south—{{p|Tamil}}, {{p|Telugu}}, {{p|Kannada}},
{{p|Malayalam}}—represent the survival of the pre-{{p|Aryan}} linguistic

Substrate, much as {{p|Basque}} preserves the pre-{{p|Indo-European}}
Substrate of {{p|Europe}}. The line between {{p|Aryan}} and {{p|Dravidian}}
India is roughly the boundary between the two great language
Families, though bilingualism, literary exchange, and cultural

Mixing have blurred it extensively over the centuries.

The {{p|Aryan Migration}} is not, in the Gaiad's reading, a catastrophe.
It is a transition—one that produced the civilization that would
Compose the {{p|Vedas}}, the {{p|Upanishads}}, the {{p|Bhagavad Gita}}, the

{{p|Mahabharata}}, the {{p|Ramayana}}, and the entire corpus of classical
{{p|Indian}} literature and philosophy. It brought {{p|Indo-European}}
Language and religion into {{p|South Asia}}. It merged with the pre-
Existing civilizations of the subcontinent to produce something

New—neither purely {{p|Aryan}} nor purely pre-{{p|Aryan}}, but a synthesis
That became, over the following centuries, one of the most
Spiritually and intellectually rich civilizations in human history.

And the parallel with the {{p|Hyksos}} holds. Both migrations happened
In roughly the same period. Both were {{c|R}}-haplogroup (the {{p|Hyksos}}
Were {{c|J2}}, not {{c|R}}; I correct myself—the parallel is structural,
Not genetic. The {{p|Hyksos}} were {{c|J2}}, the {{p|Aryans}} were {{c|R1a}}. But

Both were foreign populations entering an established civilization
At roughly the same time.) Both adopted substantial elements of
The civilizations they entered. Both were, eventually, partially
Expelled or absorbed. The {{p|Hyksos}} would be expelled from {{p|Egypt}}

By {{c|Ahmose I}} around fifteen-fifty BCE. The {{p|Aryans}} would not
Be expelled from {{p|India}}—they would merge so thoroughly with the
Pre-existing populations that no expulsion was possible. The
Demographics of {{p|India}} were permanently transformed. But both

Migrations showed the same pattern: foreign arrival, partial
Absorption, cultural transformation in both directions, and
Lasting legacy.

                   The {{p|Aryan Migration}}. The {{c|R1a}}-haplogroup
Chariot-riders entering {{p|South Asia}}. The {{p|Vedic}} hymns. The

Merging with {{p|Dravidian}} and pre-{{p|Aryan}} populations. The
Synthesis that became {{p|Hindu}} civilization. The {{p|Sanskrit}}
Language tree that includes a billion modern speakers.
The caste system as the social codification of the encounter.

{{c|Shiva}} rising from pre-{{p|Aryan}} {{p|Indus}} cultic objects into
A central deity of the new synthesis. The civilization that
Would compose the {{p|Mahabharata}} and the {{p|Bhagavad Gita}} and
The {{p|Upanishads}} is taking shape in this period.

{{p|Aryan Migration}}. {{p|India}}. The synthesis. Stand.
