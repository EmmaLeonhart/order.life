There is a sound that ecosystems make
When they are full and living—not one sound
But thousands woven into one: the wake
Of every species calling to be found

By mate or prey or rival—every frog
Competing with the cricket through the night,
Every bird above the morning fog
Proclaiming territory in the light,

Every insect drumming on the bark,
Every predator's distant cry that rolls
Across the valley through the ancient dark—
All this together fills the world in scrolls

Of meaning layered over meaning, life
On life on life in one continuous song
That is the voice of the living, the rife
And breathing world entire, all day long.

And when that voice fell silent in the Permian,
The silence was not peaceful—it was not
The comfortable quiet of meridian
Rest—it was the negative of thought,

The definition of what sound is not:
For silence that follows the superlative
Of life's own voice is not a restful spot
But rupture—the collective imperitive

Of interdependence made audible
Was gone, and what remained was wind
On bare red ground—accountable
To nothing, answered by nothing, thinned

To pure geology: the rock, the air,
The acid rain on stone, the basalt flood
Advancing on the north—and nowhere there
The rustle of a wing, the creak of bud

Opening in the morning's filtered light.
{{c|Silence}} came on foot across the plain
Where the last {{c|Cordaios}} trees still white
And acid-bleached held hollow shapes of grain-

Less wood against the sulfur-yellow sky—
She walked the forest past the leafless canopy
Where {{c|Meganeura}}'s heirs had ceased to fly,
Where no more insects lived in the canopy.

No cricket-song from any stone or bark,
No frog-call rising from the vanished pond,
No gorgonopsian's territorial mark
Of sound rang through the forest or beyond.

Then {{c|Emptiness}} moved in to fill the place
Where biodiversity had been—
Not quietly, not gently, but with face
Of everything that once was, now unseen:

For emptiness is not the absence of a thing
But the presence of its absence—you can feel
The shape of what is gone in every ring
Of silence where its voice should be the real

And breathing confirmation that the world
Still holds what it has always held—and when
That confirmation fails, the world unfurled
Reveals its fragile nature once again.

Great {{c|Mortality}} was something strange—
Not any single creature's private death
But population-level, the wide range
Of entire bloodlines losing breath:

The extinction of the possibilities—
The coral reef's specific architecture,
The fusulinid foram's mysteries
Of chamber-building, the gorgonopsian's texture

Of jaw-muscle developed over time—
These were gone not for a season or a drought
But for all seasons past the final rhyme
Of their existence: permanently out

Of the world's living inventory—
Not sleeping but deleted—not suppressed
But erased from the evolutionary story
Without revision, without redress.

{{c|Desolation}} walked the plains that had been rich
And warm and full with life through every age:
She found no niche that wasn't emptied, which
Was all of them—she walked from stage to stage

Of what had been the Permian world's display
And found each act already closed, the curtain
Drawn on forms that would not play
Again in any season—that was certain.

Only wind across the bare red ground,
Only acid rain on acid rock,
Only the basalt cooling without sound
In the north where {{c|Siberia}}'s ongoing knock

Of eruption still poured its patient black
Across the land—only these things moved
In what had been a world, and looking back
Required no effort: nothing that had loved

Or feared or eaten in the Permian
Was here to look behind it—and the stone
Would hold the record as the old guardian
Of what had been, keeping the world's own

Bone-library of the vanished forms
For those who came much later, chipped them free,
And read the testament of life's own norms
In limestone—all the biodiversity

Made permanent in mineral, in archive:
The last record of what the world could be
When it was whole and fully, richly alive—
And now the world had lost that, terrifyingly.

Honor the silence—for the silence teaches
What is vast beyond all reckoning:
The alliance of all life in one that reaches
From base to apex, the beckoning

Of the full world's voice—you cannot know
The value of the symphony until
The orchestra has stopped, until the show
Is over and the hall falls cold and still.
