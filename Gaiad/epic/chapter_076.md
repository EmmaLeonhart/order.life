Now hear the sorrow of the ancient sea
When {{c|Anoxia}} descended through
The ocean's middle depths where life ran free
And took the oxygen that living drew

From circulation's ancient gift: the cold
Dense polar water sinking toward the floor
Had always carried oxygen enrolled
In every molecule—but now no more.

For {{c|Siberia}}'s eruptions warmed the air
And warmed the ocean surface year by year,
And as the surface warmed with acid care
The polar cooling weakened—less severe

The temperature distinction top to deep
That drove the ocean's thermohaline flow:
The cold dense water failed to fall and keep
The deep-water oxygenated so.

Without the sinking cold, the circulation
Faltered—{{c|Anoxia}} moved into
The stagnant depths with patient preparation
And took the oxygen the deep once knew.

The sulfate-reducing bacteria found
Their season come at last—in low-oxygen
And sulfate-rich conditions, they abound
And make hydrogen sulfide, and begin

Their ancient chemistry of rotten-egg
And toxic-purple blooming—{{c|Hydrogen}}
{{c|Sulfide}} rose through the water-column's leg
From the deep anaerobic fen

Toward the surface, rising purple-grey
And toxic—for this gas destroys the cell
By binding to the mitochondrial way
Of energy production—it would fell

Whatever met it, breathing or in shell:
The electron transport chain shuts down,
The ATP production fails, and well
Within a breath the living creature's crown

Of function—movement, sensation, all—
Goes dark and still—the mitochondria
That every complex life-form needs to call
Its energy source, dead in the urea

Of poisoned water where the purple bloom
Of hydrogen sulfide spread its lethal tide.

And {{c|Reef Death}} came walking through the room
Of shallow-water coral gardens wide

Where symbiotic algae held the thread
Of reef construction in their photosynthesis—
But acid water bleached the algae dead
And left the coral white in cold analysis

Of its own skeleton, bare and dry—
No symbiont to feed it sugar-food,
No calcium carbonate supply
That acid hadn't leached into a crude

And porous ghost of architecture—down
The reef collapsed without its living frame:
The structure that had worn its ancient crown
Through fifty million years and never came

To any end but growth, was eaten now
From the inside by chemistry alone—
No predator, no storm—just the vow
Of acid on carbonate, eating stone.

Then {{c|Purple Waters}} spread across the sea:
The color of the anoxic tide
Tinged by the sulfur-reducing free
And blooming life that had no reason to hide

Now that the oxygen was gone—the pale
And sickly purple-grey of dying ocean
That had once been blue—the final veil
Drawn over what had been the great devotion

Of half a billion years of building up
The complex web of swimming, preying, growing—
Now chemistry alone controlled the cup
And poured its purple verdict, overflowing.

Ninety-six of every hundred kinds
Of marine life would die before the tide
Would turn—the numbers defy what mind
Can hold as real: the reefs of pride

And brachiopod in layers thick as hills,
The ammonites in their ten thousand forms,
The crinoid meadows, the fusulinid frills
Of coin-like foram—all the norms

Of Permian ocean swept away
Within a hundred thousand years of ash:
Not fast by human scale, but geologic day—
A moment, really, in the deep earth's crash.

Only the simplest stayed—the bacterial
Mat, the sulfur-feeder in the deep,
The few fish hardy in bacterialmind
And cold, who found some refuge in the steep

And cold-water pockets of the north
Where oxygen still lingered in the cold—
These few would carry the ocean's worth
Forward through the Great Dying, thin and old.

So {{c|Anoxia}} and {{c|Hydrogen Sulfide}}
And {{c|Reef Death}} walked together, drawn
By {{c|Siberia}}'s fire on every tide,
And showed us what no living thing had known:

That the ocean is not safe from what the land
Above it does—that every great eruption
Makes its acid on the sea's own hand
And breaks the ocean's chemistry's function.

The sea, vast as it has always been,
Is fragile at its chemistry's heart—
Change the temperature and intervene
In circulation, and the whole falls apart.
