In the refugia that still remained—
The deep cold-water oceanic zones,
The mountain caves where some had still retained
A temperature below the warming's tones,

The higher altitudes where thin and cold
The air still fell below the lethal line
Of wet-bulb threshold—in these few strongholds
That accident of geography made fine

Enough for life—a remnant held on still
Through what {{c|Mortality}} had closed around
The world's wide circumference—and the chill
Of accidental shelter was the ground

On which survival stood: a cave's north face
That kept its air ten degrees more cold
Than outside, a deep lake in a place
Below the surface-warming's grip of gold

And deadly heat—geography became
The school of survival for the remnant few
Who had no virtue in their winning claim
To life, only the fortune to pass through

Where the temperature still let them breathe.
{{c|Last Breath}} was what they had—not legacy
Or excellence or the deep truth beneath
All living: just the next breath—just the free

And random fact of being still alive
One generation more, one winter passed,
One breeding season barely left to thrive
In, one more year to ask if it could last.

The numbers kept declining even in
The refugia—the warming's reach
Extended year by year further in
To every cold that had been left to teach

The survivors how to stay alive:
The permafrost was thawing—methane rose—
The warming followed every mountain drive
And every depth of cave until it chose

Even the last retreats and made them less
Than what survival asked of temperature—
And so the remnant met the new distress
Of their last shelter failing, the aperture

Of possibility narrowing still.

{{c|Survivor}} was not beautiful to see—
Not any apex predator, not skill
Or size or excellence of weaponry:

Just small, and burrowing, and warm inside
Its own fur-covered body—just the plain
And barrel-bodied synapsid who'd hide
Below the acid-ground in every rain

And eat whatever vegetation managed growth
In the low-light filtered by the ash—
Who drew the next breath not by ancient oath
Of power but by cellular flash

Of metabolism kept precisely at
The rate that let the body keep its heat
Below the lethal threshold—not combat
But chemistry was the survivor's feat.

{{c|Hope}} was barely a flicker—
The flame that would not go out in the cave's
Cold dark, that kept the cells from sicker
Shutting-down than the metabolism saves

For its emergency—the spark
That five-hundred million years of living had
Built into the cellular structure's mark:
Even under everything turned bad,

The electron transport chain kept turning,
The ATP synthase kept spinning,
The proton gradient kept returning
Across the mitochondrial lining—

Life's machinery ran not because
The world was pleasant or made promises
Of continuation—only because
The mechanism fell to premises

Of direct attack, not slow temperature—
And in the refugia the kill
Was slow—too slow to stop the legislature
Of cellular continuation's will

To run the next reaction in the chain.

{{c|Endurance}} was the only teacher left—
Not wisdom, skill, nor any fine campaign
Of predator-designed—bereft

Of every advantage evolution
Builds for fitness—only the thin
And accidental circumstance's solution
Of being in the right cave to begin

The next breath, and the next, and next—
To eat the smallest beetle still alive,
To find the coolest stone when overflexed
By heat, to raise one litter and survive

One more winter—not heroism
But metabolism, not courage but
The accident of not yet reaching schism
Between alive and dead—the shut

Of the extinction door had not yet caught
These few, and they continued simply by
Continuing—and all recovery's brought
From this: from those who did not die.

Honor the last breath in the cave—
The smallest survivors, barely living—
Who held the door of life against the grave
Not by any strength but by surviving

One more hour in the cellular sense:
Keep the mechanism running, keep
The next reaction's small recompense
Of energy produced—and sleep

Only briefly, eat only what's there,
Move only when the heat drives movement—
And by these means, not brave but bare
And metabolic in their improvement

Of nothing—carry the future through
The worst the world had yet produced.
