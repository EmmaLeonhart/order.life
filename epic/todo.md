## Lineages: OPEN — Need Full Development

These are confirmed gaps. Each needs at least one dedicated chapter to tell their story properly.

| Group | Status | Geological debut | Notes |
|-------|--------|-----------------|-------|
| **Sauropsids** (reptiles → dinosaurs → birds) | Class-level only | Early Carboniferous | One of the two amniote lines from Tetrus. Lizards, turtles, crocodilians, dinosaurs, birds all descend from here. Highest priority. |
| **Synapsids** (proto-mammals → mammals) | Class-level only | Early Carboniferous | The other amniote line. Pelycosaurs → therapsids → cynodonts → mammals. Highest priority alongside sauropsids. |
| **Actinopterygii** (ray-finned fish) | Class-level only | Devonian | Ch 57 (Janusicthus) named them but gave no story. Teleosts are the most species-rich vertebrate group alive today — needs a full chapter. |

## Key Open Question

**Amniote split (Ch ~63–70 range):** Tetrus established "father of land nations" but the actual fork into the two great amniote lines hasn't been told:
- **Line A → Sauropsids** (reptiles → dinosaurs → birds)
- **Line B → Synapsids** (pelycosaurs → therapsids → mammals)

This split is the most important evolutionary story in the upcoming chapters and needs to happen soon.
